**Café Athena - Calabrian Chili & Red Pepper Cavatappi**

Smooth, emulsified pepper cream with fermented chili heat and pecorino.

Yield: 4 servings | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 25 min

**Headnote** This dish bridges the gap between a bright *arrabbiata* and a rich vodka sauce. By roasting and peeling the peppers, we remove the fibrous cellulose, ensuring the final puree is velvety rather than gritty. We finish with a strict *mantecatura*, using cold butter to stabilize the spicy emulsion against the starchy pasta water.

**Teaching Idea:** **Cellulose Management.** Pepper skins are tough and do not break down fully in a blender. Removing them is not just about flavor (avoiding char); it is about ensuring the sauce coats the ridges of the pasta without texture interruption.

**Mise En Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Roast the peppers:** Set the oven to Broil (High).
* **Steam the peppers:** Have a heat-proof bowl and plastic wrap ready.
* **Boil water:** Bring a large pot of water to a boil and season heavily with salt.
* **Prep the aromatics:** Slice onions and garlic; seed the chilis.
* **Chill the fat:** Ensure the finishing butter is cubed and kept cold in the fridge until the very last moment.

**Ingredients**

**The Vegetable Base**

* 400 g (2 large) Red Bell Peppers
* 200 g (1 medium) Yellow Onion, thinly sliced
* 10 g (2 cloves) Garlic, thinly sliced
* 30 g (2 Tbsp) Tomato Paste
* 45 ml (3 Tbsp) Olive Oil

**The Heat**

* 25 g (5 whole) Calabrian Chilis, seeded (jarred/oil-packed)
* 15 ml (1 Tbsp) Calabrian Chili Oil (from the jar)
* 2 g (½ tsp) Red Pepper Flakes (Chili Flakes)

**The Liquid & Finish**

* 473 ml (1 pint) Heavy Cream
* 454 g (1 lb) Cavatappi or Fusilli Pasta
* 14 g (1 Tbsp) Unsalted Butter, cold and cubed
* 60 g (½ cup) Pecorino Romano, finely grated
* Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt, to taste

**Method**

**Phase 1: Cellulose Removal (The Roast)** **Broil:** Coat the whole red peppers lightly in oil. Place them under the broiler until the skins are blistered and starting to char, turning occasionally. **Steam:** Transfer the hot peppers immediately to a bowl and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Let stand for **15 minutes**. **Why:** The residual heat creates steam, which separates the skin from the flesh, making peeling effortless. **Peel:** Once cool enough to handle, scrape off the charred skins with a knife. Remove stems and seeds. Slice the roasted flesh into strips.

**Phase 2: Building the Base** **Sauté:** In a large sauté pan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, warm the olive oil. Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt. Cook until softened and translucent (do not brown yet). **Caramelize:** Add the roasted pepper strips. Cook for 3–4 minutes until the onions begin to take on color. **Bloom:** Add the garlic, red pepper flakes, and seeded Calabrian chilis. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant. **Pinçage:** Stir in the tomato paste. Cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste turns a rusty brick red. **Why:** Frying the tomato paste reduces its raw acidity and develops deep umami notes through caramelization.

**Phase 3: The Emulsion** **Simmer:** Pour in the heavy cream. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for **5 minutes**, or until the cream has thickened slightly and coats the back of a spoon (*nappe*). **Blend:** Transfer the mixture to a blender. **Safety Check:** Remove the center cap of the blender lid and cover the opening with a folded kitchen towel to allow steam to escape. **Puree:** Blend on high until completely smooth and orange. Return the sauce to the pan on low heat.

**Phase 4: The Cook** **Boil:** Drop the Cavatappi into the boiling salted water. **Time:** Cook the pasta for **1 to 2 minutes less** than the package instructions. **Reserve:** Before draining, reserve 240 ml (1 cup) of starchy pasta water.

**Phase 5: The Mantecatura** **Marry:** Transfer the *al dente* pasta directly into the pan with the warm sauce. Add 60 ml (¼ cup) of pasta water. Increase heat to medium-high. **Agitate:** Toss the pasta vigorously in the sauce for 1–2 minutes. The sauce should glossy and cling to the noodles. **Mount:** Remove the pan from the heat. Immediately stir in the **cold butter** and the grated Pecorino Romano. Toss constantly until melted and emulsified. **Why:** Adding fat *off the heat* prevents the emulsion from breaking (splitting into oil and solids). **Serve:** Plate immediately, garnishing with extra Pecorino.

**Chef's Notes / Variations**

**The "Char" Variable:** If you prefer a smokier flavor, allow the peppers to turn fully black under the broiler. If you prefer a sweeter, cleaner flavor (as per this recipe), remove them as soon as the skin blisters. **Pasta Shape:** Cavatappi (corkscrews) is ideal because the ridges hold the heavy cream sauce. Fusilli or Rigatoni are acceptable substitutes. **Substitution:** If Calabrian chilis are unavailable, use 30 g of Sambal Oelek or 2 fresh Fresno chilis, though the fermentation note will be lost.

**Glossary**

**Mantecatura:** The Italian technique of vigorously tossing pasta with fat (butter/cheese) and starchy water to create a creamy emulsion. **Pinçage:** The process of cooking tomato paste in fat until it darkens/caramelizes. **Nappe:** The consistency of a sauce that is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon without running off.
