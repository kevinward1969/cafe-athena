A rich, creamy emulsion of toasted walnuts and aromatics, stabilized with a garlic-infused panade for a luxurious, velvet-like texture.

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Total:** 30 min

---

## Headnote

Traditional Ligurian walnut sauce often suffers from two flaws: the bitter astringency of walnut skins and a thin, "broken" consistency. We solve the first by oven-toasting and mechanical peeling. We solve the second by replacing the traditional soaked raw bread with a cooked panade. By sautéing baguette crumbs in olive oil with garlic and marjoram before deglazing with milk, we create a starch-fat bond that prevents the sauce from separating when hit with hot pasta water. **Teaching Idea: The Panade as a Stabilizer.** In this application, the panade acts as a physical barrier between the walnut fats and the added liquids. The hydrated starches in the bread hold onto moisture, ensuring the sauce remains creamy and "nappé" rather than oily.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Measure all ingredients:** Ensure walnuts, breadcrumbs, and liquids are weighed for precision.
- **Grate the cheese:** Finely grate the Parmigiano Reggiano using a microplane.
- **Prep the garlic:** Peel the clove and remove the germ (core) to prevent harsh bitterness.
- **Prepare the station:** Set out a clean, dry kitchen towel for peeling the walnuts.

---

## Ingredients

### The Nut Base

- 150 g (1 ½ cups) Shelled walnuts
- 30 g (⅓ cup) Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated

### The Aromatic Panade

- 30 g (½ cup) Stale baguette breadcrumbs, fine grind
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Extra virgin olive oil
- 1 clove (5 g) Garlic, peeled and cored
- 1 g (½ tsp) Dried marjoram
- 60 ml (¼ cup) Whole milk (for deglazing)

### The Emulsion & Seasoning

- 60 ml (¼ cup) Whole milk (for blending)
- 3 g (1 tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt, or to taste

---

## Method

**Phase 1: Nut Preparation (De-Skinning).**

1. **Toast:** Preheat oven to 350°F/177°C. Spread the walnuts in a single layer on a sheet pan. Toast for 7–9 minutes until the centers are pale golden and the aroma is distinctly nutty.
2. **Peel:** Immediately transfer the hot walnuts to the center of a clean kitchen towel. Fold the towel over the nuts and rub vigorously against the counter for 60 seconds. The friction will remove the majority of the papery, bitter skins (pellicles). Pick out the clean walnuts and discard the skins.

**Phase 2: The Aromatic Panade.**

1. **Sauté:** In a small skillet over medium heat, add the olive oil. Once shimmering, add the baguette breadcrumbs and the whole garlic clove. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring constantly, until the crumbs are deep golden brown and the garlic is fragrant.
2. **Bloom:** Add the dried marjoram to the skillet. Stir for 30 seconds until the herb's aroma is amplified by the hot oil.
3. **Deglaze:** Pour in the first 60 ml (¼ cup) of milk. Stir rapidly as the breadcrumbs hydrate and form a thick, pasty mass. Once the milk is fully absorbed and the mixture pulls away from the pan, remove from heat and let cool for 5 minutes.

**Phase 3: The Emulsion.**

1. **Process:** Place the skinned walnuts, the panade (including the garlic clove), and the Parmigiano Reggiano into a food processor or high-speed blender.
2. **Hydrate:** Add the remaining 60 ml (¼ cup) of milk and the salt. Pulse until the mixture reaches a thick, uniform paste. If the mixture is too dry to move, add an additional tablespoon of milk.
3. **Finish:** Taste and adjust seasoning. The paste should be salty and potent; it will be thinned further with pasta water during service.

---

## Chef's Notes / Variations

- **Service Protocol:** Never boil this sauce. To serve, place a large dollop of the paste in a warm mixing bowl. Add a splash of starchy pasta cooking water and whisk until it reaches a heavy cream consistency. Toss with hot pasta (preferably *pansotti* or *trofie*) and finish with a thread of raw olive oil.
- **Storage:** Store in an airtight container with a thin layer of olive oil on top to prevent oxidation. Remains optimal for up to 3 days in the refrigerator.

## Glossary

- **Pellicle:** The thin, papery skin surrounding a nut. In walnuts, this contains high concentrations of tannins that contribute bitterness.
- **Panade:** A mixture of starch and liquid used to provide structure or moisture to sauces and forcemeats.
- **Blooming:** The process of heating dried herbs or spices in fat to dissolve their fat-soluble flavor compounds.
- **Nappé:** A culinary term describing a sauce that is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
