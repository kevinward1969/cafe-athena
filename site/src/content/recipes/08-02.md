# Café Athena - Roasted Spaghetti Squash "Pomodoro Rustico"

Roasted vegetable pasta with a chunky, garlic-forward tomato ragout.

**Yield:** 2 mains / 4 sides | **Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Total:** ~1 hr 10 min

---

## Headnote

We replace the traditional heavy pasta sauce with a "Rustico" preparation—rapidly sautéed chunky tomatoes that coat the strands without drowning them. The squash is treated with an osmotic salt cure to ensure the "noodles" remain firm and distinct, providing a bite that mimics al dente pasta.

**Teaching Idea: Surface Area & Evaporation.** Cooking the sauce in a wide sauté pan rather than a deep pot allows water to evaporate quickly, concentrating flavor and preserving the structural integrity (chunkiness) of the tomato pieces.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Preheat** oven to 400°F/205°C.
- **Split and Scoop** the squash lengthwise; remove seeds.
- **Salt and Sweat** the squash flesh (wait 15 minutes, then pat dry).
- **Hand-Crush** the tomatoes into large, irregular chunks (if using canned) or rough dice (if fresh).
- **Slice** the garlic thinly (do not mince; we want visible slivers).

---

## Ingredients

### The Squash

- 1200 g (1 medium) Spaghetti Squash
- 28 g (2 tbsp) Extra Virgin Olive Oil
- 6 g (2 tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper

### The "Rustico" Sauce

- 55 g (¼ cup) Extra Virgin Olive Oil
- 30 g (6 cloves) Garlic, thinly sliced
- 3 g (1 tsp) Red Pepper Flakes
- 800 g (28 oz) Whole Peeled San Marzano Tomatoes
- 1 g (1 tsp) Dried Oregano (optional)
- Salt to taste

### The Finish

- 15 g (½ cup) Fresh Basil Leaves, torn
- 30 g (¼ cup) Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated

---

## Method

**Phase 1: The Osmotic Sweat** Split the squash lengthwise. Generously sprinkle the flesh with the salt. Let stand for **15 minutes**.

> **Why:** Salt is hygroscopic and pulls water out of the cells. This prevents the squash from steaming in its own liquid and becoming mushy.

**Phase 2: The Roast** You will see beads of water on the surface of the squash. Pat the flesh completely dry with paper towels. Rub with the 28 g oil and pepper. Roast **cut-side down** on a parchment-lined baking sheet for **35–40 minutes** until tender.

> **Note:** If you prefer a drier, nuttier flavor, flip them cut-side up for the last 10 minutes.

**Phase 3: The "Soffritto"** While squash roasts, add the sauce oil, sliced garlic, and red pepper flakes to a large, cold sauté pan. Turn heat to medium.

> **Why:** Starting cold allows the garlic flavor to infuse the oil (bloom) without burning. Cook until the garlic is golden brown and fragrant.

**Phase 4: The Rapid Reduction** Add the hand-crushed tomatoes and oregano to the hot oil. Increase heat to medium-high. Cook vigorously for **10–12 minutes**, stirring occasionally.

> **Goal:** You want the water to evaporate so the tomatoes "fry" in the garlicky oil. The sauce should be thick, glossy, and chunky, not watery.

**Phase 5: The Toss** Scrape the roasted squash strands directly into the pan with the simmering sauce. Toss for 1 minute to marry the flavors. Remove from heat. Fold in the fresh basil and finish with Parmigiano-Reggiano.

---

## Glossary

- **Hygroscopic:** The ability to attract and hold water molecules from the surrounding environment.
- **Blooming:** The process of briefly heating spices in fat to release essential oils.
