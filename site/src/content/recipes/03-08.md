# Café Athena - Sous Vide Chicken Liver Mousse

## A perfectly smooth, aerated forcemeat topped with a ruby port wine gelée, using thermal precision to eliminate the "grainy" texture of traditional methods.

Yield: 4–6 Ramekins (500 ml) | Prep: 40 min | Cook: 60 min | Set: 4–12 hr

**Headnote** The difference between a gritty pâté and a silky mousse is the coagulation of protein. By cooking the livers sous vide at a precise temperature, we denature the proteins just enough to set safely without tightening into hard granules. We then create a stable fat-in-water emulsion by blending the warm protein with butter. **Teaching Idea: The Emulsion Window.** Temperature is the enemy of emulsion. If the livers are too hot, the butter separates (greasy). If they are too cold, the butter solidifies before emulsifying (chunky). Both components must be warm (approx. 120°F/50°C) when they hit the blender.

**Mise En Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Preheat the Bath:** Set your sous vide circulator to 149°F/65°C.
* **Clean the Livers:** Inspect livers individually. Use kitchen shears to trim away any white connective tissue (sinew) or green bile stains (which are incredibly bitter).
* **Temper the Butter:** Cut butter into cubes and leave at room temperature. It needs to be soft, not melted.
* **Bloom the Gelatin:** Sprinkle gelatin over cold water (for the gelée) and let it hydrate for 5 minutes.

**Ingredients**

**The Aromatics**

* 50 g (¼ cup) Shallots, minced
* 1 Clove Garlic, sliced thinly
* 30 g (2 tbsp) Unsalted Butter (for sautéing)
* 60 ml (¼ cup) Cognac or Brandy
* 2 g (2 sprigs) Fresh Thyme, leaves only

**The Forcemeat**

* 450 g (1 lb) Chicken Livers, cleaned and trimmed
* 225 g (1 cup) Unsalted Butter, room temperature (cubed)
* 60 ml (¼ cup) Heavy Cream
* 8 g (1 ½ tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
* 2 g (½ tsp) Pink Curing Salt #1 (Optional, preserves pink color)
* 1 g (¼ tsp) Black Pepper, freshly ground

**The Port Gelée**

* 120 ml (½ cup) Ruby Port Wine
* 30 g (2 tbsp) Sugar
* 3 g (1 tsp) Powdered Gelatin (bloomed in 1 tbsp water)

**Method**

**Phase 1: Aromatic Reduction** In a sauté pan over medium heat, sweat the **shallots** and **garlic** in the **30 g butter** until translucent (do not brown). Add the **thyme** and **Cognac**. CAUTION: The alcohol may flame. Simmer until the liquid is almost completely evaporated (au sec). **Why:** Alcohol inhibits the setting of mousse and can taste harsh if not cooked off. We want the flavor essence, not the burn.

**Phase 2: Precision Cooking** Combine the **cleaned livers**, the **aromatic mixture**, **salt**, **pepper**, and **curing salt** (if using) in a vacuum seal bag. Seal the bag using a chamber vacuum or water displacement method. Submerge in the 149°F/65°C water bath for **60 minutes**. **Why:** This temperature ensures the livers are pasteurized and fully cooked but remain creamy pink inside.

**Phase 3: The Emulsion** Remove the bag from the bath. Open immediately. While the livers are still hot, transfer the entire contents of the bag (livers and juices) into a high-speed blender. Add the **heavy cream**. Blend on high until liquified. With the blender running on medium-low, drop in the **room temperature butter** one cube at a time. **Sensory Cue:** The mixture should look glossy and vortex smoothly. If it looks oily or separated, add a teaspoon of cold cream to bring it back.

**Phase 4: Refining and Casting** Pass the warm mixture through a fine-mesh sieve (chinois) into a clean bowl. **Why:** This removes any missed sinew or thyme stems, guaranteeing a Michelin-standard texture. Pour the mixture into ramekins, leaving ¼ inch of space at the top. Tap ramekins on the counter to release air bubbles. Refrigerate uncovered for **2 hours** until the surface is firm to the touch.

**Phase 5: The Port Gelée** In a small saucepan, warm the **Port wine** and **sugar** until the sugar dissolves. Do not boil. Stir in the **bloomed gelatin** until fully melted. Let the liquid cool to room temperature (lukewarm). **Critical:** If the jelly is too hot, it will melt the fat in the mousse and create a muddy layer. Gently pour a thin layer over the chilled mousse. Return to the fridge for **2 hours** to fully set.

**Chef's Notes / Variations**

* **Oxidation:** Without Pink Curing Salt #1 (sodium nitrite), the interior of the mousse will be pink, but the surface may turn gray where it touches oxygen before the jelly is added. This is natural.
* **Vegetarian Alternative:** Use roasted chestnuts and mushrooms in place of livers, and agar-agar for the jelly.
* **Serving:** Serve with crusty baguette and cornichons to cut the richness.

**Glossary**

* **Forcemeat:** A mixture of ground, lean meat emulsified with fat.
* **Au Sec:** "Until dry." To cook a liquid until it has nearly evaporated.
* **Bloom:** To hydrate gelatin in cold water before heating, ensuring it dissolves evenly without clumping.
