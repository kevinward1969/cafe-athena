# Café Athena - Prosciutto Balanzoni

Vibrant emerald pasta from Bologna, featuring a silky ricotta and Prosciutto di Parma farcia finished in a nutty brown butter glaze.

**Yield:** 3 servings | **Prep:** 90 min | **Inactive:** 30 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Total:** 130 min

---

### **Headnote**

Balanzoni, often called "Tortelli Matti" (Mad Tortellini), are prized for their oversized shape and deep green hue. By puréeing the spinach directly into the eggs rather than folding in chopped leaves, we achieve a perfectly homogenous, silk-like dough that handles with professional precision.

**Teaching Idea: Moisture Control.** To prevent pasta from "weeping" or bursting during the boil, filling ingredients like ricotta and sautéed greens must be processed until bone-dry. Straining the ricotta and wringing the spinach in a lint-free towel ensures the farcia remains a stable emulsion under heat.

---

### **Mise En Place**

* Finely mince the Prosciutto di Parma into a paste-like consistency.
* Strain the whole-milk ricotta through a fine-mesh sieve to remove excess whey.
* Grate the Parmigiano-Reggiano using a microplane.
* Measure the 00 flour and salt into a bowl.
* Prepare a clean, wide work surface for hand-kneading.

---

### **Ingredients**

**The Verde Pasta Dough**

* 200 g (1 ⅔ cups) 00 flour
* 100 g (approx. 2 large) eggs (room temperature)
* 50 g (approx. 1 cup packed) baby spinach
* 1 g (pinch) Diamond Crystal kosher salt

**The Salumi Filling**

* 180 g (¾ cup) whole-milk ricotta (strained)
* 70 g (2 ½ oz) Prosciutto di Parma, finely minced
* 45 g (½ cup) Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
* 15 ml (1 tbsp) heavy cream
* 1 g (¼ tsp) freshly grated nutmeg
* black pepper to taste

**The Beurre Noisette Finish**

* 60 g (4 tbsp) unsalted butter
* 30 g (¼ cup) Parmigiano-Reggiano (for finishing)

---

### **Method**

**Phase 1: The Emerald Base**

1. **Prep the Spinach:** In a pan over medium heat, sauté the baby spinach until fully wilted. Transfer to a lint-free towel and squeeze until bone-dry. **Why:** Removing all moisture is critical to preventing the dough from becoming gummy or sticky during the kneading process.
2. **Purée the Base:** In a high-speed blender, combine the eggs and the bone-dry spinach. Process until a smooth, vibrant green liquid forms without any visible leaf fragments.
3. **Hydrate the Flour:** On the work surface, form a "volcano" with the 00 flour. Pour the green liquid into the center and gradually whisk flour from the inner walls using a fork until a shaggy mass forms.
4. **The Knead:** Knead by hand for 8–10 minutes. **Sensory Cue:** The dough should feel as soft as an earlobe and bounce back slowly when poked. If it sticks to your hand, add a light dusting of flour.
5. **The Rest:** Wrap tightly in plastic and let rest at room temperature (approx. 70°F/21°C) for 30 minutes. **Why:** Resting allows the gluten network to relax, making the dough extensible enough to roll to paper-thinness without snapping back.

**Phase 2: The Salumi Farcia**

1. **Build the Emulsion:** In a medium bowl, whisk together the minced prosciutto, strained ricotta, Parmigiano, heavy cream, and nutmeg until homogenous. **Sensory Cue:** The filling should be stiff enough to hold its shape on a spoon and should not "weep" any liquid.

**Phase 3: Shaping the Balanzoni**

1. **The Roll:** Roll the dough through a pasta machine to setting #6 or #7 (approx. 1.5mm). Cut into 10cm (4-inch) squares.
2. **The Seal:** Place 1 heaping teaspoon of filling in the center of each square. Fold diagonally into a triangle, pressing firmly from the center outward to remove all air. **Why:** Trapped air will expand in the boiling water, causing the pasta to burst.
3. **The Twist:** Join the two bottom corners of the triangle around your index finger and press firmly to seal, creating the signature "jumbo tortellino" shape.

**Phase 4: The Finish**

1. **Toast the Butter:** In a wide skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Continue cooking until the foaming subsides and it smells of toasted hazelnuts (**Beurre Noisette**).
2. **The Boil:** Cook the Balanzoni in boiling water (100°C/212°F) for 3–4 minutes until they float and the edges are tender.
3. **The Glaze:** Transfer the pasta directly into the butter with a small splash of pasta water. Toss gently over low heat until the sauce reaches **Nappé**. Serve immediately with extra cheese.

---

### **Glossary**

* **Beurre Noisette:** "Hazelnut butter." Butter cooked until the water evaporates and the milk solids toast, creating a nutty aroma.
* **Farcia:** A professional term for a fine pasta stuffing or emulsified meat filling.
* **Nappé:** The consistency where a sauce coats the back of a spoon and a clear line remains when a finger is drawn through it.
