A structural, multi-variant foundation for creating edible "stained glass" vessels and tiles using high-protein cheese matrices and moisture-controlled inclusions.

**Yield:** 12–18 units | **Prep:** 45 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Total:** 1 hr

## Headnote

This document is the "Construction Site" of the larder. We are building structural components rather than simple garnishes. By manipulating the cheese matrix, we control the tensile strength, color, and snap of the frico, allowing for everything from a delicate herb-laminated tile to a rigid, high-walled vessel. **Teaching Idea: Thermal Plasticity.** Between 150°F/65°C and 110°F/43°C, the frico is in its "plastic" phase, where it can be draped, folded, or cut with geometric precision. Once it drops below 100°F/38°C, it enters the "glass" phase—any attempt to shape it will result in shattering.

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

* **Stabilize Inclusions:** Complete the **01-20 Hydro-Thermal Stabilization** process for all inclusions in advance — they must be bone-dry before you begin.
* **Matrix Prep:** Microplane all cheeses into a fluffy pile. Do not use a standard box grater; we need a high surface-area "snow" for an even, lacy melt.
* **Shape Station:** Arrange molds (rocks glasses, shot glasses, or pipes) and metal ring/cookie cutters on a cool work surface.
* **Tool Setup:** Ensure a clean non-stick pan and a thin offset spatula are ready at the stove.

## Ingredients

### The Base Matrix (Variant 1: The Control)

* 200 g (2 cups) Parmigiano-Reggiano, microplaned
* 50 g (½ cup) Irish Dubliner, microplaned

## Method

### Phase 1: The Base Layer

1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat.
2. Sprinkle a thin, uniform layer of the **Cheese Matrix** (approx. 1 tbsp) into your target shape (circle or square).
3. **Sensory Cue:** Wait for the cheese to begin "webbing" and bubbling, but before it reaches a deep golden brown.

### Phase 2: Lamination (The Sandwich)

1. Lay your stabilized **Laminate** (herb leaf, carrot shape, or cabbage) flat onto the melting cheese.
2. Immediately "dust" a second, very light layer of the matrix over the inclusion.
3. **Why:** This "sandwiches" the vegetable, frying it in the cheese fat and locking it into the protein structure to ensure it becomes part of the matrix rather than a loose garnish.

### Phase 3: The Final Render

1. Cook for 60–90 seconds until the bubbling slows and the edges turn a uniform golden brown.
2. **Sensory Cue:** Slide an offset spatula under the edge; the entire disc should lift as one rigid, lace-like sheet without drooping.

### Phase 4: Shaping Variants (The 20-Second Window)

Choose your geometry immediately upon removal from heat.

* **Option 1: The Flat Tile (Geometric Cut):** Lay the frico flat on a cool surface. Within 5 seconds, use a metal ring or cookie cutter to punch out a clean shape. Remove scraps while still warm.
* **Option 2: The Molded Vessel (Vase/Cup):** Drape the disc over a rocks glass (large) or shot glass (mini). Press a second glass over it to set the pleats and form a cup.
* **Option 3: The Structural Tube:** Wrap the disc around a cannoli mold or clean pipe to create a cylinder.

## Chef's Notes / Variations

* **Carrot Geometric Shapes:** To achieve the look of the basil in reference images, use a small aspic cutter on mandolined carrot slices *before* blanching.
* **Flatness Protocol:** For perfectly flat tiles, place a second Silpat and a weighted sheet pan on top of the fricos as they cool to the glass phase.
* **Flavor Stacking:** Mimolette (Variant C) pairs exceptionally well with purple cabbage for high-contrast visual and earthy flavors.
* **Scrap Utility:** Do not discard geometric off-cuts; they provide a "broken glass" garnish with concentrated umami flavor.

## Glossary

* **Hydro-Thermal Stabilization:** The combined use of heat and pressure to prep inclusions for fat-based cooking.
* **Thermal Plasticity:** The state where cheese fat is pliable before crystallization.
* **Lamination:** Layering material (cheese-inclusion-cheese) to create a reinforced composite structure.
* **Matrix:** The structural medium (cheese) that holds the laminates.
