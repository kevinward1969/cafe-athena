The architecture of lamination, creating thousands of crisp, shattered layers through mechanical leverage.

**Yield:** ~1.2 kg (2.5 lbs) | **Prep:** 1 hr | **Inactive:** 4 hr | **Cook:** N/A

---

## Headnote

True puff pastry is a marvel of physics, not chemistry. There is no yeast or baking powder; the lift comes entirely from water in the butter flashing into steam, pushing apart the 729+ layers of dough.

**Teaching Idea: Plasticity Matching.** The most critical factor in lamination is ensuring the *détrempe* (dough) and the *beurrage* (butter block) have the exact same consistency. If the butter is too hard, it tears the dough; if too soft, it squirts out the sides.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Chill the workstation:** If your kitchen is warm (above 72°F/22°C), chill your countertop with ice bags before starting.
- **Temper the butter:** Remove butter from the fridge 20 minutes before starting the *beurrage*. It should be pliable but cool.
- **Prepare the water:** Dissolve the salt and vinegar in the cold water and keep it in the fridge until the exact second of use.
- **Measure the guides:** Have a ruler ready. Precision in rectangle size determines the evenness of your layers.

---

## Ingredients

### The Détrempe (Dough Encasing)

- 500 g (4 cups) All-Purpose Flour
- 50 g (3½ tbsp) Unsalted Butter, melted and cooled
- 10 g (2 tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) White Vinegar or Lemon Juice
- 225 g (1 cup minus 1 tbsp) Ice Water

### The Beurrage (Butter Block)

- 450 g (1 lb) European Style Butter (82%+ Fat), cool room temperature
- 40 g (⅓ cup) All-Purpose Flour

---

## Method

**Phase 1: The Détrempe (The Envelope)** In a large bowl, combine the flour and the melted butter. Rub it in with your fingertips until it resembles coarse meal. Make a well in the center. Pour in the cold water/salt/vinegar mixture. Mix gently with a fork or dough scraper until a shaggy mass forms. Knead briefly (5–6 times) just to bring it together. Do not overwork. Pat into a 6x6 inch square, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for **1 hour**.

> **Why:** This relaxes the gluten, preventing the dough from fighting you during the rolling process.

**Phase 2: The Beurrage (The Engine)** While the dough rests, place the cool butter and flour on parchment paper. Pound with a rolling pin to soften without melting. Form into a perfect 5-inch square block of even thickness. Wrap and refrigerate for 15 minutes.

> **Sensory Cue:** Press the dough and the butter. They should dent with the exact same amount of finger pressure.

**Phase 3: The Lock-In** Roll the chilled *détrempe* into a 10-inch square (twice the size of the butter). Place the butter block in the center, rotated 45 degrees (like a diamond on a square). Fold the corners of the dough over the butter to meet in the center, sealing the seams tightly. You now have a square package.

**Phase 4: The Turns (Lamination)**

**Turn 1:** Roll the package into a long rectangle (approx. 8x20 inches). Dust with minimal flour. Fold the top third down and the bottom third up (like a business letter). This is a "Single Turn."

**Turn 2:** Rotate the block 90 degrees (so the "book opening" is on your right). Roll and fold again.

**Rest:** Mark the dough with two fingerprints (to track turns). Wrap and chill for **1 hour**.

> **Why:** The gluten has tightened; resting prevents shrinkage and ensures the layers stay distinct.

**Phase 5: Completion** Repeat the rolling and folding process for two more sets of two turns (resting 1 hour between each set), for a total of **6 Single Turns**. After the final turn, wrap tightly and chill for at least 4 hours (preferably overnight) before using.

---

## Chef's Notes / Variations

- **The Acid Variable:** The vinegar in the *détrempe* relaxes the gluten proteins and prevents the dough from oxidizing (turning gray) during the long resting periods.
- **Rough Puff (Demi-Feuilletée):** For a faster version, cube the cold butter and toss it directly into the flour (leaving large chunks intact), then add water and proceed immediately to rolling. It yields 80% of the height with 20% of the effort.
- **Freezing:** This dough freezes exceptionally well. Cut the finished block into 4 portions, wrap in double plastic, and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge.

---

## Glossary

- **Détrempe:** The base ball of dough that encases the butter in laminated pastries.
- **Beurrage:** The prepared block of butter (often mixed with a little flour) used for lamination.
- **Plasticity:** The property of a solid fat to be shaped or molded without breaking or melting.
