Golden, saffron-infused rice utilizing the "White Beef" stock technique.

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Total:** 40 min

## Headnote

Classic Milanese often uses chicken stock, but this method uses **White Beef Stock** to provide a meaty backbone that stands up to the floral saffron. We also employ the "Grated Onion" technique — sweating the onion into a puree so it provides sweetness without interrupting the creamy texture of the rice. **Teaching Idea: Mantecatura.** The secret to silky risotto is not stirring during cooking — it's the vigorous off-heat beating of cold butter and cheese into the starch. Resting the risotto for one full minute before beating allows the fats to temper gently, producing a stable emulsion rather than a broken, oily sauce.

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

* **Infuse the saffron:** Add saffron threads to the cold stock and set the pot over heat; the infusion happens as it comes up to temperature.
* **Grate the Onion:** Peel and grate the yellow onion on the fine side of a box grater.
* **Cube the Butter:** Cut the cold butter into cubes and return to the fridge (it must be cold for the finish).
* **Grate the Cheese:** Finely grate the Parmesan.

## Ingredients

**The Base**

* 1 yellow onion, peeled and grated
* 400 g (2 cups) Carnaroli rice (or Arborio)
* 125 ml (½ cup) dry white wine (Pinot Grigio)
* 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

**The Liquid**

* 1.5 L (6 cups) White Beef Stock (or Chicken Stock + 1 Beef Stock Pot/Cube)
* 1 generous pinch saffron threads

**The Finish (Mantecatura)**

* 100 g (3.5 oz) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
* 60 g (2 oz) Parmesan cheese, finely grated

## Method

**Phase 1: Infuse the Stock.** Bring stock to a simmer over medium heat. Add the saffron threads and let steep for 5 minutes to release color. Keep warm on low heat throughout cooking.

**Phase 2: The Invisible Base.** In a heavy-bottomed pot (Dutch oven), heat the olive oil and a small knob of the butter over low heat. Add the **grated onion** and a pinch of salt.

* **Technique:** Sweat gently for 3–5 minutes. Do not brown. The onion should turn into a melting puree.
* **Why:** This eliminates the texture of chopped onions, ensuring the final risotto is perfectly smooth.

**Phase 3: Toast and Deglaze.** Add the rice to the pot. Stir constantly for 2 minutes.

* **Sensory Cue:** The grains should become hot to the touch and coated in fat ("pearl-like"). Add the white wine. Stir until fully evaporated and the pan is "dry."

**Phase 4: The Feed.** Begin adding the hot, saffron-infused stock, one or two ladles at a time. Stir frequently to massage the starch out of the rice. Keep the heat medium-high; the stock should be bubbling lively, not just soaking. Cook for 16–18 minutes until *al dente* (tender but with a slight bite).

**Phase 5: The Rest (Mantecatura).** When the rice is cooked and slightly fluid (not stiff), turn off the heat. Add the cold cubed butter and the Parmesan. **Do not stir yet.**

* **The Rule:** Let it rest for 1 minute.
* **Why:** This allows the fats to temper gently. If you stir immediately, the emulsion might break or become oily. After 1 minute, beat the risotto vigorously with a wooden spoon to emulsify. Serve immediately.

## Chef's Notes / Variations

* **The White Beef Stock:** A pale, unroasted beef stock made from raw bones and blanched aromatics. It delivers full beef flavor without the dark color that would muddy the saffron's golden hue. A good substitute is high-quality chicken stock reinforced with a beef stock cube.
* **Rice Selection:** Carnaroli is preferred over Arborio for its higher starch content and firmer grain, which holds up better through the long cooking process.

## Glossary

* **Mantecatura:** The final step of beating cold butter and cheese into risotto off the heat to create a creamy emulsion.
* **Sweat:** To cook vegetables in fat over low heat so they soften and release moisture without browning.
* **Al Dente:** "To the tooth." Pasta or rice that is cooked through but retains a slight firmness in the center.
* **Grated Onion:** The onion, peeled and grated on a fine grater, is cooked slowly to create a smooth puree, contributing sweetness and texture without disrupting the risotto's creaminess.
* **Pearls:** Refers to the individual grains of rice that have been heated and coated in fat, achieving a glossy, uniform appearance during the risotto cooking process.
* **Infuse:** To steep a flavor ingredient, such as saffron threads, in a liquid (stock) to extract its essence and impart its color and aroma.
* **Emulsify:** To combine two immiscible liquids (like butter and stock) into a stable mixture, typically achieved through vigorous agitation.
