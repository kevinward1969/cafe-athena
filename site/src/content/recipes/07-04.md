# Café Athena - Beef Bourguignon (The Elevated Short Rib Braise)

A modernized, gelatin-fortified stew featuring a "sacrificial" wine reduction and bone-in short ribs.

Yield: 6 servings | Prep: 45 min | Cook: 3.5–4 hr | Total: ~5 hr

### **Headnote**

By borrowing the classical "sacrificial vegetable" technique, we infuse the wine with aromatics before it ever touches the beef, eliminating the harsh alcohol bite often found in quick reductions. We reinforce the sauce body with a controlled bloom of gelatin to mimic the lip-smacking texture of a restaurant-quality veal stock without the need for excessive commercial thickeners.

**Teaching Idea:** **Sacrificial Aromatics.** Vegetables cooked in a braise for 3 hours lose all texture and flavor to the sauce. To maximize flavor, we use one set of chopped vegetables to flavor the wine reduction (which are discarded/sacrificed), and a second set of fresh vegetables added at the very end for service.

### **Mise en Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Temper and Brine:** Cut meat into 5cm (2-inch) cubes. Pat completely dry and season generously with salt 45 minutes prior to cooking.
* **Bloom the Gelatin:** Sprinkle the gelatin powder over the cold beef stock and allow it to hydrate for 5 minutes.
* **Prep the Reduction:** Roughly chop the leeks, shallots, and reduction carrots.
* **Prep the Garnish:** Cut bacon into lardons (rectangles). Clean mushrooms. Peel pearl onions.

### **Ingredients**

**The Protein**

* 2.5 kg (5.5 lbs) Boneless Short Ribs or Beef Chuck Roast
* 20 g (2 tbsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
* 5 g (1 tsp) Black Pepper

**The Wine Reduction (Sacrificial)**

* 750 ml (1 bottle) Dry Red Wine (Pinot Noir or Cabernet)
* 150 g (1 cup) Leeks, white part only, washed and sliced
* 100 g (½ cup) Shallots, roughly chopped
* 100 g (½ cup) Carrots, roughly chopped
* 50 g (½ cup) Mushroom stems (reserved from garnish)

**The Braise Base**

* 115 g (4 oz) Slab Bacon, cut into lardons (¼" x 1" strips)
* 1000 g (4 cups) Beef Stock, cold
* 7 g (1 packet) Powdered Gelatin (Knox)
* 25 g (1 tbsp) Tomato Paste
* 1 Head Garlic, halved horizontally
* 5 Sprigs Fresh Thyme
* 2 Bay Leaves

**The Garnish**

* 300 g (10 oz) Cremini or Button Mushrooms, caps only
* 300 g (10 oz) Carrots, oblique cut (large bite-sized chunks)
* 200 g (1 cup) Pearl Onions, peeled (fresh or frozen)
* 57 g (¼ cup) Unsalted Butter, cold

### **Method**

**Phase 1: The Wine Reduction** Combine the **Red Wine** and the **Sacrificial Vegetables** (leeks, shallots, chopped carrots, mushroom stems) in a saucepan over medium-high heat. Simmer until the liquid is reduced by approximately 40% (about 15–20 minutes). Strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve and discard the vegetables. **Why:** Boiling the vegetables in the wine extracts their sugars and aromatics while evaporating the alcohol, creating a complex base that won't taste "boozy" in the final dish.

**Phase 2: The Render & Sear** Place the **Bacon Lardons** in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Cook until the fat is fully rendered and the bacon is crisp. Remove bacon with a slotted spoon and reserve. Increase heat to medium-high. Working in batches, sear the **Beef** in the hot bacon fat until deeply browned on all sides. Remove beef and set aside.

**Phase 3: The Fond & Deglaze** Add the **Tomato Paste** to the remaining fat in the pot. Cook for 1 minute until it darkens to a rust color (*pincage*). Pour in the **Strained Wine Reduction**. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up the dark crust (*fond*) from the bottom of the pot.

**Phase 4: The Braise** Return the beef to the pot. Add the **Beef Stock** (with bloomed gelatin), **Garlic**, **Thyme**, and **Bay Leaves**. Bring to a simmer, cover tightly, and transfer to a 150°C (300°F) oven. Cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender but not falling apart.

**Phase 5: The Velvet Finish** Remove the pot from the oven. Use tongs to transfer the beef to a clean bowl. Strain the sauce into a separate container, pressing on the solids to extract all liquid; discard the spent herbs and garlic. Skim off excess fat. Return the liquid to the pot and bring to a simmer. Add the **Pearl Onions** and **Oblique Carrots**. Simmer for 15 minutes until tender.

**Phase 6: The Garnish** While the sauce reduces, sauté the **Mushroom Caps** in a separate pan with a splash of oil until golden brown. **Why:** Cooking mushrooms separately prevents them from becoming slimy in the sauce.

**Phase 7: Assembly** When the sauce coats the back of a spoon (*nappe*), whisk in the cold **Butter** to emulsify. Add the cooked beef, crispy bacon, and sautéed mushrooms back into the pot. Gently fold to coat everything in the glaze. Serve immediately.

### **Glossary**

* **Sacrificial Aromatics:** Vegetables used solely for flavor extraction in a stock or sauce base, intended to be discarded rather than eaten.
* **Lardons:** Strips of slab bacon or salt pork, usually poached or rendered to create a cooking fat.
* **Pincage:** The technique of cooking tomato paste in fat until it caramelizes/darkens, removing acidity and adding depth.
* **Nappe:** The consistency of a sauce that is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon and hold a line when drawn through with a finger.
