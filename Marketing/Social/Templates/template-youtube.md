## Template A — Long-Form Technique Video
**Pillar 1: Technique Foundations | Target length: 12–25 min**

*Direct video translation of a technique folio — the principle explained, demonstrated, and applied to at least one recipe from the book. Dense, no padding. These rank long-tail and compound over time.*

---

### Title Formula

```
[TECHNIQUE NAME] — [WHAT IT IS, HOW IT WORKS, AND WHY IT MATTERS]
```

*Formula rules:*
- Lead with the technique keyword — this is the primary search term
- The phrase after the dash answers what the video actually delivers
- No "ultimate," "easy," "game-changing," or any modifier that makes a promise the video cannot keep
- Length: 60–80 characters

*Examples:*
- `The Fond — What It Is, How It Forms, and Why It Changes a Sauce`
- `Brown Stock — The Method, the Mirepoix, and How to Read a Finished Stock`
- `Emulsification — How Sauces Hold Together and What Breaks Them`
- `Pasta Structure — Hydration, Gluten Development, and Why the Dough Rests`

## Template B — Short Explainer Video
**Pillar 2: Why It Works | Target length: 6–10 min**

*Shorter standalone videos built around a single culinary question. Entry points for Persona 2 — search-driven, high-value for the algorithm because they satisfy intent completely. Each video should answer the question fully.*

---

### Title Formula

```
Why [FOOD THING] [WORKS / DOES X] (And What's Actually Happening)
```

*Alternative formulas:*
- `What [TECHNIQUE/INGREDIENT] Actually Does — And Why It Matters`
- `[COMMON COOKING QUESTION]? Here's the Answer.`
- `The Reason [TECHNIQUE] Works — Explained Simply`

*Formula rules:*
- The question or claim in the title must be fully answered in the video — no bait
- "What's Actually Happening" is the correct register: curious, not condescending
- Lead with the search term (pasta water, brown butter, Maillard reaction, etc.)
- Length: 55–75 characters

*Examples:*
- `Why Pasta Water Works in a Sauce (And What's Actually Happening)`
- `What the Maillard Reaction Actually Is — And When It Matters`
- `Why Fat Temperature Controls Pastry Texture`
- `What a Fond Is and Why It Changes Every Pan Sauce`

---

### Description Block

```
[PARAGRAPH 1 — 1–2 sentences: State the question this video answers. Direct. No tease. Persona 2 will close the tab if the description doesn't deliver the premise immediately.]

[PARAGRAPH 2 — 2–3 sentences: Name the specific mechanics covered. What does the starch do, what does the salt do, what happens at what temperature. These are the secondary search terms and the reason a careful viewer watches twice.]

[PARAGRAPH 3 — 1–2 sentences: Related folio or recipe on the site. Where the full written version lives.]

---

[RELATED FOLIO/RECIPE NAME] at cafeathenathemanual.com/[SLUG]

Café Athena — cafeathenathemanual.com
```

*Example:*

```
Pasta water works in a sauce because of what it carries — starch from the pasta, salt from the cook, and temperature. This video explains exactly what each of those elements is doing and why the timing matters.

The starch acts as an emulsifier — it stabilizes the fat-and-water interface in the sauce. The salt is already dissolved, so it integrates without graining. The temperature keeps the emulsion mobile; once it cools below a threshold, it breaks. Demonstrated in a cacio e pepe and a pasta al burro.

The pasta folios at cafeathenathemanual.com have the full written method for both dishes.
```
