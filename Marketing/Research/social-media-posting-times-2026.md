Instagram
Best days.

Multiple 2026 datasets agree mid‑week is strongest, with Wednesday and Thursday consistently top; Friday and Saturday are weakest for engagement.

Best times (global averages).

Buffer’s 9.6M‑post study: Thursday 9 a.m., Wednesday 12 p.m., and Wednesday 6 p.m. are the highest‑engagement times; evening posts (6–11 p.m.) outperform mornings most days.

Other aggregators (Brandwatch, Metricool, Deep Wire) find midweek 7–9 a.m. and 11 a.m.–1 p.m. as reliable secondary windows.

Format nuances.

Reels: early morning (6–8 a.m.) and evening (7–9 p.m.) perform best; longer discovery window means timing matters mainly for initial boost.

Feed posts (images/carousels): most sensitive to timing; post when your followers are online (midweek, mornings + lunch + evenings).

Stories: morning and evening (roughly 7–10 a.m. and 7–10 p.m.); posting twice per day increases total views.

YouTube
Best days.

Synthesized 2025–26 analyses show Friday–Sunday as best overall, with Thursday a strong “warm‑up” day for new uploads.

Best times.

A data‑backed guide finds Friday–Sunday between 2–5 p.m. local time yields the most views in the first 48 hours; weekend videos generated up to 41% more early views than Monday/Tuesday.

Saturday: 9 a.m.–12 p.m. is prime for how‑to and educational content; Sunday: 11 a.m.–2 p.m. for longer deep‑dives.

Strategy notes.

Several studies recommend publishing 2–3 hours before your audience’s peak activity so processing and initial distribution complete before they log on.

Shorts can perform slightly earlier in the day (late morning–afternoon), while long‑form thrives in afternoon/early evening when people have time for longer sessions.

Pinterest
Best days.

Sprout‑based summaries: planners are most active Mon–Wed during workday lulls; Tue–Wed 10 a.m.–1 p.m. score highest for many business accounts.

Other large‑scale analyses find engagement peaks on weekends, with Saturday and Sunday evenings (7–11 p.m.) as the top global windows.

Best times.

Daytime planner windows: Mon 10 a.m.–12 p.m.; Tue 10 a.m.–1 p.m.; Wed 8 a.m.–1 p.m. for pinning fresh content as people plan projects.

Evening browsing windows: 8–11 p.m. local time delivers 2–4× more engagement across multiple studies, especially on Sunday–Tuesday.

Late night (11 p.m.–1 a.m.) is surprisingly strong for core demographics like young mothers and working women in some datasets.

Niche timing (useful for recipes/DIY).

For recipes/food, 4–6 p.m. (dinner planning) and weekend evenings perform best; for home decor/DIY, Saturday 10 a.m.–2 p.m. is ideal; productivity/planners peak Sunday evening.

Facebook
Best days.

Aggregated 2026 data from Buffer, Sprout Social, Hootsuite, and SocialPilot: Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are consistently strongest; Saturday and Sunday are weakest.

Best times.

Crowbert’s synthesis: early mornings (5–9 a.m. ET) and mid‑day (12–3 p.m. ET) are the most reliable engagement windows; weekends show no strong peaks.

Buffer’s 14M‑post analysis highlights Thursday at 9 a.m. as the single best slot, with Wednesday the highest‑engagement day overall and Saturday worst.

Sprout Social reports a broad Tue–Wed 12–8 p.m. window as high‑engagement, especially for business pages.

Practical posting pattern (audience local time).

Aim for 7–9 a.m. and 11 a.m.–2 p.m. on Tuesday–Thursday for important posts; use Monday/Friday mornings for lighter content; keep weekend posts to late morning and lower‑stakes.

How to find your best times
These “global best times” only explain part of performance (recent meta‑analyses put timing at roughly 10–20% of a post’s ceiling); content quality, format, and audience fit do the rest.

Use built‑in analytics.

Check “When your followers are online” in Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, and YouTube Studio by day and hour; prioritize slots where your followers cluster.

Run a 2–3 week timing experiment.

Pick 2–3 candidate windows per platform (e.g., midweek noon vs evening) and post comparable content; compare reach, watch time, saves, and click‑through.

Segment by content type.

Educational/coding content and longer videos often do best on weekend afternoons and Sunday; quick tips, Reels, and shorts can thrive in midweek evenings.

Anchor to audience time zone and habits.

Always schedule to your audience’s predominant time zone, and adjust for whether they’re students, professionals, or parents (commutes, lunch breaks, and evening downtime all shift by segment).
