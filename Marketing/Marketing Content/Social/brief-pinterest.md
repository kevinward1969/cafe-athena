## Purpose

Pinterest is a long-tail search engine, not a social feed. Its value for Café Athena is passive and compounding: a well-pinned folio or technique post continues to surface in search results for months or years after it's posted. The channel's primary function is organic discovery — connecting search intent ("how does emulsification work," "what is a fond," "braised chicken thighs method") directly to Café Athena content.

Secondary function: acts as a visual index of the site, with each pin linking back to a specific recipe or technique folio at `cafeathenathemanual.com`. Every pin is a persistent entry point.

Pinterest does not build a community. It builds traffic. Treat it accordingly — optimize for search, not for engagement.

---

## Primary Audience

**Persona 2 — The Culinary Curious** is the best fit for Pinterest's search behavior. She searches for specific techniques and ingredients when something didn't work or when she wants to understand something she encountered. She discovers content through search, not feeds. A well-titled pin with a technique keyword in the description lands in her results organically.

Persona 3 (The Kitchen Experimenter) also uses Pinterest in research phases — particularly when she's in a deep dive on a cuisine or technique category. She's less likely to follow an account than to save individual pins across multiple boards.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. Technique Folios

Each technique folio in The Manual gets a dedicated pin. Title = folio name. Description includes the central principle and two or three specific technique keywords. Links to the folio page on the site. These pins are the highest-value long-term assets — technique searches are consistent and high-intent.

### 2. Recipe Singles

One pin per recipe. Dish name in the title. Description leads with what makes this recipe structurally interesting (the technique, the principle, the thing the headnote explains) rather than a list of flavors. Links to the recipe page.

### 3. Boards

Three boards, organized by content type. Every pin is assigned to exactly one board. See Board Routing below.

### 4. Process Frames

Pins that document mid-cook moments — the fond before deglazing, pasta dough before lamination, a reduction at the right stage. These rank for procedural queries ("what does a reduction look like when it's ready") and drive traffic from cooks actively troubleshooting. Fewer of these initially — add as the image library grows.

## Posting Cadence

**3 scheduled pins per week** from the content calendar, plus backfill as the archive grows.

| Day | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | EXPO post pin — linked to recipe article on site | 8–11pm |
| Saturday | Technique infographic + document pin (NotebookLM) | 8–11pm |
| Sunday | Recipe video pin (repurposed from Instagram reel) | 8–11pm |

**Backfill strategy:** Pinterest rewards consistent activity over time. In parallel with the weekly cadence, systematically pin existing folios and recipes — one technique folio + one or two recipe pins per session until the full archive is covered. After backfill, new content pins go live at the same time as the site page.

No seasonal or trend-chasing content. The catalog is permanent; the pins should be too.
