## Template A — Technique / Knowledge Post

**Pillar 1: The Why Behind a Step | Posts Monday**

---

### Hook Line

```
[ONE SENTENCE THAT NAMES THE PRINCIPLE — not a question, not a tease. A statement that rewards the reader for stopping.]
```

*Examples:*

- `Most recipes say "soften the onions." Here is what soften actually means.`
- `The pan needs to be dry before the fat goes in. This is why.`
- `A reduction is ready when it coats the back of a spoon and a line holds. This is the test, not a timer.`

*Voice rule: Short declarative sentence. No "did you know" or "have you ever wondered." State the fact; the curiosity is already there.*

---

### Body (3–5 lines)

```
[STAGE 1 — NAME WHAT IS HAPPENING]
[1–2 sentences: the physical or chemical process behind the step. Specific, not general.]

[STAGE 2 — WHY THE COOK IS STOPPING WHERE THEY ARE]
[1–2 sentences: what happens if you go further, what you lose if you stop short. Give the decision logic.]

[STAGE 3 — HOW IT CARRIES FORWARD]
[1 sentence: where this principle shows up again — the next dish, the next technique. Connect it to something beyond this one recipe.]
```

*Voice rule: Write as someone who has worked through the material and found the logic in it. No hedging. No reassurance. Active construction, present tense.*

---

### CTA

```
The full [FOLIO/RECIPE TITLE] is at the link in bio. [OPTIONAL: One sentence on what else the folio covers.]
```

*Examples:*

- `The full Stocks & Fond folio is at the link in bio. It covers the fond before the liquid goes in, skimming, and reading a finished stock.`
- `Technique Folio 01-01 is at the link in bio. Knife skills, mise en place, and the logic behind both.`

*Voice rule: No "check out," no "head over to." State what's there and where it is.*

---

### Hashtag Block (15–20 tags)

```
[TECHNIQUE-SPECIFIC TAG] [DISH/INGREDIENT TAG] [STATION TAG]

#[TECHNIQUE] #[DISH] #[STATION]
#cafeathena #cookbooklife #culinarytechnique #homecooking #howtocook
#foodscience #kitchenprinciples #learntocock #seriouseats #cookathome
#[CUISINE TYPE] #[INGREDIENT] #cheftechnique #saucework #homechef
```

*Instructions: Lead with the technique keyword and dish name — these carry the search weight. Follow with brand tags, then general food-education tags. Cap at 20. No trend tags, no seasonal tags.*

## Template B — Visual / Dish Post

**Pillar 4: The Table | Posts Wednesday**

---

### Hook Line

*(Optional — this pillar can open directly into the body. If used, keep it under 10 words.)*

```
[DISH NAME — PLAIN. OR: ONE SENTENCE ABOUT WHAT THIS WAS.]
```

*Examples:*

- `Pasta al burro — what we had on Tuesday.`
- `Brown butter. One pan. The whole sauce.`
- *(blank — let the image open the post)*

---

### Body (2–4 lines)

```
[SENTENCE 1: What the dish is — no promotion, no enthusiasm. State it plainly.]
[SENTENCE 2: One structural observation — the technique, the principle, the thing the headnote would say. Not a flavor description.]
[OPTIONAL SENTENCE 3: Where this dish lives in the book, or what it connects to.]
```

*Examples:*

- `Pasta al burro. Brown butter after the milk solids caramelize at around 150°C — the aromatics you get from whole butter don't exist below that threshold. The recipe is on the site.`
- `Braised chicken thighs, finished in the fond. The liquid that looked like nothing becomes the sauce once you add the stock and reduce it down.`

*Voice rule: No simulated warmth. No "cozy," "perfect," "amazing." Describe the dish and say one true thing about it.*

---

### Hashtag Block (3–5 tags only)

```
#[DISH OR INGREDIENT] #[TECHNIQUE OR STATION] #cafeathena #[CUISINE TYPE]
```

*Instructions: This pillar uses a minimal tag set. The visual carries the post — the hashtag block should not overwhelm it. 3–5 tags maximum. No hashtag dump on visual posts.*

---

*Note on image standard: Natural light. Warm surfaces (cream, parchment). No elaborate plating or garnish theatre. Every image should look like it came from the same kitchen — because it did.*
