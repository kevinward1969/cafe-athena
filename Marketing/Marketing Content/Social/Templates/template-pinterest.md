## Template A — Technique Folio Pin
**Pillar 1: Technique Folios | Board: [STATION NAME]**

*These are the highest-value long-term assets. Technique searches are consistent and high-intent. Optimize title and description for the search query a cook actually types.*

---

### Title (keyword-optimized, 50–100 chars)

```
[TECHNIQUE NAME] — [THE CENTRAL PRINCIPLE IN ONE PHRASE]
```

*Formula: Lead with what the technique is, then specify what the folio teaches. Write the title as the search query someone types when they want to understand this technique — not the folio's internal name.*

*Examples:*
- `How to Build a Brown Stock — The Method Behind the Fond`
- `Knife Skills and Mise en Place — The Setup That Makes Everything Else Work`
- `Emulsification — Why Sauces Break and How to Keep Them Together`
- `Pasta Structure — Hydration, Gluten, and Why the Dough Rests`

*Voice rule: No superlatives ("the ultimate," "perfect," "best"). No promise the folio cannot keep. State what it covers.*

---

### Description (2–3 sentences, technique keywords woven in)

```
[SENTENCE 1: Name the central principle — the structural thing this technique is actually about. Include the technique keyword naturally.]
[SENTENCE 2: Name 2–3 specific sub-skills, stages, or decisions the folio covers. These are the secondary search terms.]
[SENTENCE 3: Close with the site attribution. No exclamation points.]
```

*Examples:*
- `The fond — the caramelized protein and sugar deposits that develop during roasting — is the difference between a stock and a good stock. This folio covers the full method: roasting, mirepoix, bouquet garni, long extraction, and skimming. Café Athena — cafeathenathemanual.com`
- `Emulsification is what keeps a vinaigrette from breaking and what holds a beurre blanc together. The folio explains the mechanics — fat-to-water ratio, temperature, emulsifier role — and demonstrates the technique in two sauces. cafeathenathemanual.com`

*Voice rule: Technical terms used correctly and introduced when needed. No hedging ("sort of," "basically," "kind of"). Write as someone who has worked through this and knows what to name.*

## Template B — Recipe Single Pin
**Pillar 2: Recipe Singles | Board: [STATION NAME]**

*One pin per recipe. Lead with what makes it structurally interesting — the technique, the principle, the thing the headnote explains — not a flavor list.*

---

### Title (keyword-optimized, 50–100 chars)

```
[DISH NAME] — [THE TECHNIQUE OR PRINCIPLE THAT DEFINES IT]
```

*Formula: Dish name anchors search. The phrase after the dash is what a cook types when they're trying to understand why something works, not just how to make it.*

*Examples:*
- `Pasta al Burro — How Brown Butter Changes a Sauce`
- `Braised Chicken Thighs — Reading the Fond Before You Add the Liquid`
- `Pâte Brisée — What Fat Temperature Does to Pastry Structure`
- `Cacio e Pepe — Why Pasta Water Works as an Emulsifier`

---

### Description (2–3 sentences, technique keywords woven in)

```
[SENTENCE 1: Name the structural thing that makes this recipe worth understanding — the technique, the chemical process, the decision the headnote explains. This is not a flavor description.]
[SENTENCE 2: Note what this technique applies to beyond this recipe — where the principle carries forward. Gives Persona 3 something to use.]
[SENTENCE 3: Site attribution and link. Direct, no flourish.]
```

*Examples:*
- `Brown butter isn't butter with color — it's butter after the milk solids have caramelized at around 150°C, producing aromatics that whole butter can't deliver. The structural technique behind this recipe applies to anything you'd finish with butter. Full recipe at cafeathenathemanual.com.`
- `The fond left in the pan after searing is not a cleanup problem — it's the base of the sauce. This recipe uses the braising liquid and a reduction to turn it into something. cafeathenathemanual.com`

---

### Board Assignment

```
[STATION BOARD NAME]
```

*Use the same board taxonomy as Template A. Recipe pins go to the station where that dish lives in the manuscript. A pasta dish goes to `The Saucier` or `The Entremetier` depending on how the technique is weighted.*

---

### Link

```
cafeathenathemanual.com/[RECIPE SLUG]
```

*Example: `cafeathenathemanual.com/04-01` for the pasta al burro recipe*
