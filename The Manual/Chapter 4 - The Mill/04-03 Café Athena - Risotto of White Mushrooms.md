# Café Athena - Risotto of White Mushrooms

A study in texture: silky rice with separately sautéed "folded in" mushrooms.

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Total:** 40 min

## Headnote

Cooking mushrooms inside the rice for 20 minutes can make them rubbery and slimy. This recipe teaches the "Separate Sauté" technique: browning the mushrooms hard in a separate pan and folding them in at the very end to preserve their meatiness and clean flavor. **Teaching Idea: Moisture Management in Mushrooms.** Mushrooms are 90% water. Added raw to a simmering risotto, they release that water and steam rather than brown, resulting in a pale, rubbery texture. Searing them in a screaming hot separate pan drives off the moisture first — Maillard browning can only happen once the surface water is gone.

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

* **Process the Mushrooms:** Clean and slice the mushrooms paper-thin.
* **Grate the Onion:** Peel and grate the onion on the fine side of a box grater.
* **Prep the Herbs:** Pick and finely chop the fresh parsley or chervil.
* **Warm the Stock:** Bring chicken stock to a simmer.

## Ingredients

**The Mushroom Component**

* 400 g (14 oz) white button mushrooms, cleaned and sliced thin
* 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil (for sautéing)

**The Rice Base**

* 1 yellow onion, peeled and grated
* 400 g (2 cups) Carnaroli rice (or Arborio)
* 125 ml (½ cup) dry white wine
* 1.5 L (6 cups) chicken stock (high quality or stock pot fortified)

**The Finish**

* 100 g (3.5 oz) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
* 60 g (2 oz) Parmesan cheese, finely grated
* 15 g (2 tbsp) fresh flat-leaf parsley or chervil, chopped

## Method

**Phase 1: The Rice Base.** In a heavy-bottomed pot, sweat the **grated onion** in a splash of oil until melting. Add the rice and toast for 2 minutes. Deglaze with white wine and cook until dry. Begin adding the hot chicken stock in ladles, stirring frequently, until the rice is *al dente* (approx. 16–18 minutes).

**Phase 2: The Mushroom Sauté (Separate Pan).** While the rice cooks, heat a separate skillet with olive oil over **high heat**. Add the sliced mushrooms. Sauté vigorously until deep golden brown.

* **Note:** Season with salt only *after* they brown to prevent drawing out moisture too early.
* **Optional:** Add a splash of stock to the skillet to glaze the mushrooms, then turn off the heat.

**Phase 3: The Fold.** When the risotto is ready, remove the pot from the heat. Fold in the sautéed mushrooms (and any pan juices). Add the cold butter and Parmesan. **Rest for 1 minute.**

**Phase 4: Emulsify and Serve.** Beat the risotto vigorously to create the creamy emulsion. Stir in the fresh chopped herbs right before serving to keep the color bright green.

## Chef's Notes / Variations

* **Mushroom Variety:** White button mushrooms are specified for their clean, neutral flavor that lets the rice shine. For a more intense result, substitute 50% cremini and add a small handful of dried porcini (rehydrated, liquid strained and added to the stock).
* **The Herb:** Chervil is the traditional Milanese choice — delicate and anise-like. Flat-leaf parsley is a more accessible substitute.

## Glossary

* **Mantecatura:** The final step of beating cold butter and cheese into risotto off the heat to create a creamy emulsion.
* **Fold:** To gently combine a delicate ingredient (like sautéed mushrooms) into a heavier mixture without crushing it.
* **Deglaze:** Adding liquid to a hot pan to dissolve the fond (browned bits).
* **Al Dente:** Indicating the desired texture of cooked rice, meaning "to the tooth," representing a firm, yet yielding consistency.
* **Sauté:** A cooking technique involving cooking food quickly in a small amount of fat over relatively high heat.
* **Fond:** The browned bits deposited on the bottom of a pan during high-heat cooking, contributing significantly to flavor.
