### **Café Athena - Louisiana Blackened Chicken Pasta**

Spicy, smoke-kissed poultry over pasta in a stabilized Cajun cream reduction.

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 30 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Total:** ~55 min

## Headnote

This dish is an exercise in fond management. The flavor foundation is built by searing spice-crusted chicken until a deep crust forms on the pan, then aggressively lifting that flavor with wine and aromatics. Penne carries the heavy sauce well, but the recipe is fully modular and scales to fresh gnocchi beautifully. **Teaching Idea: The Stabilized Reduction.** Traditional cream sauces split when acidic ingredients like wine or tomatoes are added. Here, cream cheese acts not just as a thickener but as an emulsifying agent — its casein proteins and gums hold the wine, cream, and rendered fat in a glossy, velvet suspension that clings to the pasta without breaking.

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Pound the chicken:** Place breasts between plastic wrap and pound to an even ½-inch thickness.
- **Mince and dice:** Brunoise the onions and bell peppers so they melt into the sauce. Mince the garlic.
- **Blend the rub:** Whisk all rub spices together in a small bowl.
- **Temper the dairy:** Bring heavy cream and cream cheese to room temperature (70°F/21°C) to prevent seizing.
- **Grate the cheese:** Microplane the Parmesan finely.

## Ingredients

### The Blackening Rub

- 12 g (2 tbsp) Cajun seasoning (low-sodium preferred)
- 3 g (1 tsp) onion powder
- 3 g (1 tsp) garlic powder
- 2 g (1 tsp) black pepper, freshly cracked
- 1 g (1 tsp) dried parsley
- 2 g (1 tsp) smoked paprika
- 1 g (½ tsp) Old Bay seasoning

### The Protein

- 450 g (2 breasts) boneless skinless chicken breast, pounded flat
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)

### The Aromatics

- 150 g (1 small) white onion, fine dice
- 150 g (1 medium) bell pepper (red or green), fine dice
- 120 g (4 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
- 10 g (2 cloves) garlic, minced
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry white wine (Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio)

### The Sauce

- 480 g (2 cups) heavy cream (36% fat)
- 240 g (1 cup) chicken stock (see *10-01 Fond Blanc*)
- 113 g (4 oz) cream cheese, cubed and at room temperature
- 50 g (½ cup) Parmesan cheese, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) Cajun seasoning (reserved for the sauce)

### The Pasta

- 454 g (16 oz / 1 box) penne rigate, OR a batch of *04-11 Master Potato Gnocchi*

## Method

**Phase 1: The Blackening.** Generously coat the pounded chicken on both sides with the rub, pressing the spices into the meat. Heat the neutral oil in a large sauté pan or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Lay the chicken into the pan away from you and cook undisturbed for 4–5 minutes until a dark (but not burnt) crust forms. Flip and cook for 3–4 minutes until internal temperature reaches 155°F/68°C (carryover will bring it to 165°F/74°C). Remove to a board to rest. **Do not wipe the pan.**

**Phase 2: Scrape the Fond.** Reduce the heat to medium and add a splash more oil if the pan is dry. Add the onions and bell peppers and sauté for 3–4 minutes, scraping up the dark fond left by the chicken with a wooden spoon. Add the minced garlic and drained tomatoes and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the white wine and let it bubble aggressively, scraping the bottom of the pan, until the liquid reduces by half (*au sec*). **Why:** the alcohol must evaporate to remove its sharp raw flavor, leaving only the bright acidity to cut the cream's richness.

**Phase 3: The Emulsion.** Pour in the chicken stock and heavy cream. Bring to a gentle simmer. Whisk in the cubed cream cheese and the reserved 1 tsp of Cajun seasoning. Simmer on low for 5–7 minutes until the sauce coats the back of a spoon (*nappé*). Remove from heat and whisk in the Parmesan until smooth. Taste for salt — Cajun blends are often salty, so adjust last.

**Phase 4: The Assembly.** While the sauce reduces, boil the penne in heavily salted water until *al dente*. Drain, reserving ½ cup of pasta water. Slice the rested chicken against the grain into strips. Add the pasta directly to the sauce pan and toss vigorously to coat — if the sauce is too thick, loosen with a splash of pasta water. Fold in the sliced chicken or fan it over the top for presentation.

## Chef's Notes / Variations

- **The Gnocchi Upgrade.** This sauce pairs exceptionally well with fresh gnocchi. Replace the penne with **Parisian Gnocchi (04-08)** or **Master Potato Gnocchi (04-11)**. Boil the gnocchi until they float, then transfer them directly into the simmering sauce with a spider strainer and let them simmer in the sauce for 1 minute to absorb flavor before serving.
- **Spice Modulation.** The heat in this dish comes from the cayenne in the Cajun blend. For a milder version, reduce the Cajun seasoning in the rub by 50% and rely on smoked paprika for color and depth.
- **Cream Cheese Selection.** Use only block-style cream cheese, never tub or whipped. Tub varieties contain stabilizers and added air that disrupt the emulsion.

## Glossary

- **Fond:** The browned bits of protein and sugars stuck to the bottom of a pan after searing. A concentrated source of flavor.
- **Au Sec:** "Almost dry." Reducing a liquid until nearly all moisture has evaporated, concentrating flavor and acidity.
- **Nappé:** The consistency of a sauce thick enough to coat the back of a spoon and hold a clean line when drawn through with a finger.
- **Brunoise:** A very fine dice (1–2 mm cubes), used here so aromatics melt into the sauce rather than remaining textural.
