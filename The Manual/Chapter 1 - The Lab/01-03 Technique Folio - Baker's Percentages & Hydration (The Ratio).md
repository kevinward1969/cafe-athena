## **Technique Folio - Baker's Percentages & Hydration (The Ratio)**

**Concept Definition** Baker's Percentages are a notation method where every ingredient is expressed as a percentage of the total flour weight. Unlike standard recipes where everything adds up to 100%, in baking, the **flour is always 100%**. All other ingredients (water, salt, yeast) are calculated relative to the flour.

**The Science: Hydration Structure** Baking is chemistry. The ratio of water to flour (**Hydration**) dictates the structure of the crumb (the inside of the bread).

* **Low Hydration (50–60%):** Tight, dense crumb (e.g., Bagels, Pretzels). The gluten network is stiff and holds its shape easily.
* **High Hydration (75–85%+):** Open, airy, irregular crumb (e.g., Ciabatta, Sourdough). The excess water creates steam during the "oven spring," expanding the gluten network violently to create large holes.

**Practical Application: The Universal Formula.** Using percentages allows you to scale a recipe up or down perfectly without doing complex math.

* **The Formula:** `(Weight of Ingredient / Total Weight of Flour) × 100 = %`.
* **Example:** If you have 1000g of flour and you use 750g of water, you have a **75% hydration** dough.

**Practice Exercise: The "Scaling" Test**

**Goal:** Create a simple rustic loaf using a 70% hydration formula.

1. **Start with Flour:** Measure 500g Bread Flour. (This is your 100%).
2. **Calculate Water (70%):** 500g × 0.70 = 350g Water.
3. **Calculate Salt (2%):** 500g × 0.02 = 10g Salt.
4. **Calculate Yeast (1%):** 500g × 0.01 = 5g Instant Yeast.
5. **Action:** Combine, mix, and observe how this hydration level feels compared to a standard sandwich bread (usually 60%).
