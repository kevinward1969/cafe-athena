### **Technique Folio - Sourdough Stewardship (The Levain vs. The Mother)**

**Concept Definition:** The biggest mistake home bakers make is treating their sourdough starter as a single, static ingredient. It is not. You must distinguish between **The Mother** (your storage library) and **The Levain** (the active ingredient). Baking directly from a jar that has been sitting in the fridge for a week is the culinary equivalent of putting cold, old oil into a race car engine.

**The Science: Acid Load & Proteolysis** To understand why we build a "Levain" (an off-shoot) before baking, we must look at acidity.

* **The pH Curve:** As a starter ferments, bacteria produce acetic and lactic acid. Over time, the pH drops significantly (becoming very acidic).
* **Gluten Degradation:** Acidity weakens gluten. If you add a large amount of highly acidic, old "Mother" starter to your dough, the acid will sever the gluten bonds you are trying to build. This is called **Proteolysis**.
* **Result:** The dough becomes sticky, tears easily, and refuses to hold its shape. The bread will be flat, dense, and unpleasantly sour.
* **The Fix:** A "Levain" is a young, sweet pre-ferment. By taking a tiny bit of the Mother and feeding it a large amount of fresh flour, you dilute the acidity and maximize the yeast population just as they hit their peak activity.

**Practical Application: Ratios & Temperature**

**1. The Maintenance Ratio (Storage Mode)**

* **Ratio:** 1:1:1 (Starter : Water : Flour)
* **Use:** When keeping the Mother alive in the fridge.
* **Why:** This provides just enough food to keep the colony dormant but viable for 5–7 days at cold temperatures.

**2. The Strengthening Ratio (Preparation Mode)**

* **Ratio:** 1:5:5 or even 1:10:10.
* **Use:** 12 hours before you plan to bake.
* **Why:** If you feed a starter a massive meal (5x its weight in food), it forces the yeast to reproduce aggressively to conquer the new territory. This reduces acidity and boosts lifting power.

**3. The "Hooch" Indicator**

* **Observation:** A layer of grey/black liquid on top of your refrigerated starter.
* **Meaning:** This is alcohol produced by fermentation. It means the colony has consumed *all* available food and is starving.
* **Action:** Pour it off (for a milder flavor) or stir it in (for a sharper sourness), then feed immediately.

**Practice Exercise: The "Peak Watch"**

* **Goal:** Identify the exact moment of maximum lifting power.
* **Setup:** Take 20g of starter. Feed it 20g water and 20g flour in a clear, straight-sided glass (not a bowl). Place a rubber band at the starting level.
* **Action:** Watch it every hour.
  * **Hours 1–3:** Slow movement (Lag Phase).
  * **Hours 4–6:** Rapid expansion (Log Phase).
  * **The Peak:** The top will dome, then flatten.
  * **The Fall:** The dome collapses, and streaks appear on the glass.
* **Lesson:** You must mix your dough *at the Peak* or slightly before. Once it falls, the yeast is exhausted, and the acid load is too high.
