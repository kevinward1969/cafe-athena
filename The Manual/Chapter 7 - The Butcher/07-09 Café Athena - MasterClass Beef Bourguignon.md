## Glossary

* **Braising:** A slow, moist-heat cooking method involving sealing meat and then simmering it submerged in liquid, resulting in tender, flavorful results.
* **Red Wine:** A wine characterized by its color ranging from ruby to garnet, typically fermented without oxygen to retain color and tannins.
* **Pearl Onions:** Small, tightly clustered onions, often peeled and blanched before cooking, providing a delicate sweetness and texture.
* **Thyme:** An aromatic herb with needle-like leaves and a strong, earthy flavor, commonly used to season meats and vegetables.
* **Stock:** A flavorful liquid made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables, concentrating their essence and forming the base of many dishes.
* **Mushroom:** A fungus with a fleshy stem and cap, offering a range of flavors from earthy to delicate, and often used in sauces and stews.
* **Maillard Reaction:** A chemical reaction between amino acids and reducing sugars that occurs during high-heat cooking, producing complex flavors and aromas.
