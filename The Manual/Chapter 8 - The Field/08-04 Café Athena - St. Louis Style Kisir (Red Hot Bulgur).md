# Café Athena - St. Louis Style Kisir (Red Hot Bulgur)

Spicy, tomato-infused grain salad with a smoky BBQ kick.

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 20 min | **Hydration:** 15 min | **Total:** 35 min

---

## Headnote

This recipe fuses the mechanics of a traditional Turkish *Kisir* with the flavor profile of St. Louis BBQ. By blooming "Red Hot Ripplets" seasoning in warm oil and tomato paste, we create a "deconstructed BBQ sauce" that is absorbed directly into the fine bulgur wheat. The result is a sticky, savory, and spicy starch that acts as the perfect acidic foil to rich smoked meats.

**Teaching Idea: Grain Absorption.** Unlike rice or pasta, Fine Bulgur (Burghul #1) does not need to be boiled. It is small enough to fully hydrate simply by sitting in hot liquid, preserving its distinct chew and preventing mushiness.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Boil the Water:** Bring a kettle of water to a boil.
- **Chop the Herbs:** Finely slice the scallions and chop the parsley; keep them separate.
- **Measure the Spice:** Have the Red Hot Ripplets seasoning ready by the stove.
- **Prepare the Bowl:** Place the dry bulgur in a large heat-proof mixing bowl.

---

## Ingredients

### The Grain Base

- 150 g (1 cup) Fine Bulgur Wheat (Burghul #1)
- 180 ml (¾ cup) Boiling Water

### The "Red Hot" Bloom

- 60 ml (¼ cup) Extra Virgin Olive Oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) Tomato Paste
- 15 g (2 tbsp) Red Hot Ripplets Seasoning (adjust to heat preference)

### The Acid & Fresh

- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Pomegranate Molasses
- 1 Lemon, juice only
- 4 Scallions (Green Onions), thinly sliced
- 15 g (¼ cup) Fresh Parsley, finely chopped

---

## Method

**Phase 1: The Bloom** In a small saucepan over **low heat**, combine the olive oil and tomato paste. Stir constantly for 2–3 minutes until the oil turns orange and the paste smells sweet (not metallic). Remove from heat immediately. Stir in the Red Hot Ripplets Seasoning.

> **Why:** Adding the seasoning *off the heat* prevents the sugar in the BBQ rub from burning, while the residual heat releases the fat-soluble chili oils (capsaicin).

**Phase 2: The Hydration** Pour the hot oil-paste mixture directly over the dry bulgur in the mixing bowl. Use a fork to stir vigorously until every grain is coated in red oil. Pour the **boiling water** over the mixture. Stir once to distribute. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap or a lid. Let sit undisturbed for **15 minutes**.

> **Why:** The grain swells and absorbs the spicy tomato water, locking the flavor inside the kernel rather than just coating the outside.

**Phase 3: The Fluff and Dress** Remove the lid. The bulgur should be tender and most liquid absorbed. Fluff the grains with a fork to break up any large clumps. Drizzle with the pomegranate molasses and fresh lemon juice. Toss to combine.

> **Sensory Cue:** The texture should be slightly tacky and hold together when pressed, not dry and loose like rice.

**Phase 4: The Fresh Fold** Allow the mixture to cool for 5 minutes (so it doesn't wilt the herbs). Fold in the sliced scallions and fresh parsley. Taste and adjust seasoning—add more lemon juice if the pork is very fatty and you need more acid to cut through it. Serve at room temperature.

---

## Chef's Notes / Variations

- **Texture Warning:** This dish is *supposed* to be sticky and dense, similar to a couscous cake or tartare. If you prefer a loose salad, reduce the water by 2 tablespoons.
- **Pomegranate Molasses:** This is a thick syrup made from reduced pomegranate juice. It provides a tart, tannic sweetness that mimics the base of a good BBQ sauce. In a pinch, you can substitute a balsamic glaze.
- **The Heat Variable:** Red Hot Ripplets seasoning is potent. If serving to heat-sensitive guests, start with 1 tablespoon and add more at the end if needed.

---

## Glossary

- **Blooming:** The process of briefly heating spices in fat to release essential oils and amplify flavor.
- **Burghul #1:** The finest grind of cracked wheat. It cooks instantly in hot water and is the standard for *Kisir* and *Tabbouleh*.
- **Kisir:** A traditional Turkish salad made from fine bulgur, tomato paste, pomegranate molasses, and herbs.
