# Café Athena - Sesame Tuile Cylinders (Noir & Orange)

Delicate, lace-like sesame snaps rolled around a savory-sweet miso and orange dark chocolate ganache.

**Yield:** 12–15 cylinders | **Prep:** 30 min | **Cook:** 10–12 min | **Total:** ~1 hr

---

## Headnote

These cylinders are technically a hybrid of a *Tuile* and a *Florentine*. The high ratio of sugar and honey creates a batter that spreads aggressively in the oven, bubbling to create a distinct lace pattern before setting into a glass-like snap. We balance this intense sweetness with a "Noir" filling: dark chocolate bridged with Red Miso (umami/salt) and dried orange peel (brightness).

**Teaching Idea: The Plasticity Window.** Sugar-based doughs have a very short window of malleability. Between 160°C and 60°C, the sugar is fluid. Once it drops below 50°C, it crystallizes instantly. You must shape the cookies during this 30-second window—too hot and they slump; too cold and they shatter.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Prep the Forms:** Set up your cannoli metal forms and a wire rack.
- **Bloom the Peel:** Combine the heavy cream and dried orange peel granules in a small saucepan.
- **Chop the Chocolate:** If using block chocolate, chop into small, uniform shards to ensure even melting.
- **Preheat:** Set oven to 325°F/163°C. Line two baking sheets with silicone mats (Silpat) or parchment paper.

---

## Ingredients

### The Shell Base

- 50 g (3.5 tbsp) Unsalted Butter
- 50 g (¼ cup) Granulated Sugar
- 20 g (1 tbsp) Honey
- 20 g (2.5 tbsp) All-Purpose Flour
- 40 g (¼ cup) Sesame Seeds (Black and White mix)
- 1 g (1 pinch) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt

### The Noir Filling

- 100 g (3.5 oz) Dark Chocolate (60–70% Cacao)
- 100 g (scant ½ cup) Heavy Cream
- 15 g (1 tbsp) Red Miso (Aka Miso)
- 5 g (1 tsp) Dried Orange Peel Granules
- 20 g (1.5 tbsp) Unsalted Butter, room temperature

---

## Method

### Phase 1: The Infused Ganache

1. **Bloom:** Heat the heavy cream and orange peel granules in a saucepan over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let sit for 5 minutes.

   > *Why: This hydrates the dried peel and extracts the essential oils into the dairy fat.*

2. **Dissolve:** Whisk the Red Miso into the warm cream until fully dissolved.

3. **Emulsify:** Place the chopped chocolate in a heat-proof bowl. Pour the hot cream mixture (including peel) over the chocolate. Let stand for 1 minute, then whisk from the center outward until glossy.

4. **Finish:** Whisk in the 20 g of room temperature butter until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap touching the surface and refrigerate until pipeable (approx. 30–45 mins).

### Phase 2: The Batter

1. **Melt:** In a small saucepan, combine the 50 g butter, sugar, and honey. Heat gently, stirring constantly, just until the butter is melted and sugar is dissolved. Do not boil.

2. **Bind:** Remove from heat. Stir in the flour and salt until smooth.

3. **Incorporate:** Fold in the sesame seeds. The batter will be loose and pourable.

### Phase 3: Bake and Shape (The Critical Step)

1. **Pipe:** Spoon or pipe the batter onto the baking sheet, spaced 4 inches apart. Pipe a thick line or zigzag about **1.5 inches wide**—this ensures the cookie spreads enough to wrap fully around the cannoli tube.

2. **Bake:** Bake for 10–12 minutes. The cookies will bubble violently and spread. They are done when the entire surface is a deep golden amber (color equals crunch).

3. **The Roll:** Remove from the oven. **Wait 15–30 seconds.**

   > *Sensory Cue:* Touch the edge with an offset spatula. If it is liquid, wait. If it lifts as a cohesive sheet, it is ready.

4. **Form:** Lift a cookie, flip it onto the counter (bottom side up), and immediately roll it around a cannoli tube. Press the seam against the counter for 10 seconds to seal. Slide the tube out once rigid.

### Phase 4: Assembly

1. **Pipe:** Transfer the chilled ganache to a piping bag.
2. **Fill:** Pipe the ganache into the cooled sesame cylinders. Serve within 4 hours for maximum crispness.

## Glossary

- **Plasticity:** The quality of being easily shaped or molded. In sugar work, this depends entirely on temperature.
- **Tuile:** French for "tile." A thin, crisp wafer, often curved, named after the curved roof tiles of French country homes.
- **Ganache:** An emulsion of chocolate and liquid (usually cream).
