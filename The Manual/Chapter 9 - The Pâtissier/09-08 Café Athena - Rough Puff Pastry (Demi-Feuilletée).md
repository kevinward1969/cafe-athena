# Café Athena - Rough Puff Pastry (Demi-Feuilletée)

The "Cheat's Puff." A rapid lamination technique that creates a flaky, shattered texture perfect for sausage rolls, galettes, or quick mille-feuille.

**Yield:** ~1 kg (2.2 lbs) | **Prep:** 20 min | **Inactive:** 1 hr | **Cook:** N/A

---

## Headnote

While classic puff relies on a precise "lock-in" of a butter block, Rough Puff relies on the **Blitz Method**. We purposefully under-mix the butter, leaving large, visible chunks (marble-sized) in the dough.

**Teaching Idea: The Butter Mosaic.** When you roll out this chunky dough, the butter pieces flatten into long, thin ribbons. These ribbons separate the dough layers, creating the steam pockets necessary for lift, mimicking the structure of true lamination without the labor.

---

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- **Chill everything:** Place the mixing bowl and flour in the freezer for 15 minutes before starting.
- **Cube the butter:** Cut the butter into large 2cm (¾ inch) cubes. Keep them frozen until the last second.
- **Prepare ice water:** Dissolve the salt in the water and keep it on ice.

---

## Ingredients

### The Dry Mix

- 500 g (4 cups) All-Purpose Flour
- 10 g (2 tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
- 50 g (¼ cup) Granulated Sugar (optional, include only for sweet applications)

### The Fat & Liquid

- 450 g (1 lb / 4 sticks) Unsalted Butter, frozen cubes
- 200–225 g (¾ cup – 1 cup) Ice Water
- 5 ml (1 tsp) Lemon Juice or Vinegar

---

## Method

**Phase 1: The "Blitz" Mix** Place the flour and salt in a chilled large bowl. Add the frozen butter cubes. Toss the butter in the flour to coat, then press each cube flat between your thumb and finger *once*. **Do not rub.** You want large, flat shards of butter, not a meal.

> **Why:** If the butter becomes too small, it will incorporate into the dough (like a biscuit) rather than creating distinct layers.

**Phase 2: Hydration** Make a well in the center. Add the lemon juice to the ice water, then pour 200 g (¾ cup) of the liquid into the well. Mix gently with a dough scraper or stiff spatula, folding the flour over the liquid. Add the remaining water only if dry flour pockets remain. The dough should be shaggy and barely holding together.

**Phase 3: The Rough Rolls (Lamination)** Dump the messy pile onto a lightly floured surface. Pat it into a rough rectangle.

**Turn 1:** Roll the dough into a long rectangle (approx. 6x15 inches). The edges will be jagged; this is normal. Fold the top third down and the bottom third up (Letter Fold).

**Turn 2:** Rotate the dough 90 degrees. Roll and fold again immediately.

> **Why:** Because the butter started frozen, you can usually perform the first two turns without resting. If the butter feels soft/greasy, stop and chill.

**Phase 4: Rest and Finish** Wrap the dough in plastic and refrigerate for **30 minutes**.

**Turns 3 & 4:** Remove from the fridge. Perform two more "Roll and Folds." Wrap and chill for at least **1 hour** before using.

> **Sensory Cue:** By the final turn, the dough should look smooth, with visible streaks of yellow butter running through the white dough.

---

## Chef's Notes / Variations

- **Temperature Control:** The success of this dough depends entirely on the butter remaining solid. If it melts, you lose the layers. If the kitchen is hot, chill the dough between *every* turn.
- **Use Case:** This is excellent for savory pies, sausage rolls, or Tarte Tatin. It is *not* recommended for applications requiring extreme, uniform vertical lift (like a Vol-au-Vent); use Classic Pâte Feuilletée (09-05) for that.
- **Storage:** This dough keeps for 3 days in the fridge or 3 months in the freezer.

---

## Glossary

- **Demi-Feuilletée:** "Half-Leaf." A French term for simplified puff pastry methods.
- **Blitz Method:** A mixing technique where fat is tossed into flour and left in large pieces before liquid is added, speeding up the lamination process.
- **Letter Fold:** A standard folding technique where dough is folded in thirds, like a business letter.
