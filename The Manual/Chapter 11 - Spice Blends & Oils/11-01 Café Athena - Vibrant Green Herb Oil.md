# Café Athena - Vibrant Green Herb Oil

Stabilized chlorophyll extraction for finishing.

Yield: 240 ml (8 fl oz) | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 10 min | Total: 12 hr 40 min

---

## Headnote

This preparation represents a mechanical extraction of chlorophyll and fat-soluble aromatics rather than a standard infusion. By rupturing cell walls into a neutral carrier oil, we achieve a vibrant visual impact suitable for high-level plating. **Teaching Idea:** Blanching the herbs deactivates polyphenol oxidase, the enzyme responsible for enzymatic browning, ensuring the oil maintains its bright pigment for extended storage.

---

## Mise en Place

- Prepare a large pot of boiling water seasoned with Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt and a corresponding ice bath (50% ice, 50% water).
- Set up a high-speed blender.
- Line a sieve with a coffee filter and set over a clean deli container.
- Weigh the oil and herbs according to metric specifications.

---

## Ingredients

### Herb Component

- 75 g (2.5 cups) flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
- 75 g (1.5 cups) chives, roughly chopped

### Oil Component

- 300 ml (1.25 cups) grapeseed oil or neutral canola oil

---

## Method

**Phase 1: Thermal Shock.**

Plunge the parsley and chives into the boiling water for exactly 15 seconds. Immediately transfer to the ice bath to halt the cooking process.

> Why: This short exposure deactivates browning enzymes and sets the chlorophyll without degrading volatile flavor compounds.

**Phase 2: Dehydration.**

Once cold, remove herbs from the ice bath and squeeze firmly with a clean towel to remove all possible moisture. Rough chop the resulting herb "puck."

> Why: Water is the primary enemy of this preparation; excess moisture promotes bacterial growth and prevents a clear final product.

**Phase 3: Mechanical Extraction.**

Combine the blanched herbs and the oil in the blender and process on high for 3–5 minutes.

> Sensory Cue: Monitor friction heat; stop when the canister feels hot to the touch (approx. 140°F/60°C) or steam begins to appear.

> Why: Controlled heat helps rupture the final cell walls, allowing the oil to dissolve the maximum amount of pigment.

**Phase 4: Gravity Clarification.**

Pour the mixture into the prepared filter setup. Transfer to the refrigerator and allow to drain for 12 hours. Do not press or squeeze the solids.

> Why: Gravity ensures a clear oil while trapping fibrous solids; pressing would force particles through and result in cloudiness.

---

## Glossary

- **Polyphenol Oxidase:** An enzyme in plant cells that causes browning when exposed to oxygen; heat deactivates it.
- **Shear:** The mechanical force applied by blender blades to tear apart solid particles and disperse them into the liquid.
