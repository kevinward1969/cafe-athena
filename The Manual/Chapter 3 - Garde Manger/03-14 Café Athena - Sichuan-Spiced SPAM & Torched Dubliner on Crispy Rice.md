**Café Athena - Sichuan-Spiced SPAM & Torched Dubliner on Crispy Rice**

Crispy rice platforms layered with dijonnaise, seared Korean SPAM, a cracked three-pepper crust, and torched Irish Dubliner — finished with dressed bitter greens and sesame.

**Yield:** 14–16 pieces | **Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Total:** 35 min (assumes crispy rice platforms are prepared)

## Headnote

This is a deliberate fusion canapé built on contrast: the numbing, floral heat of Dahongpao Sichuan pepper cuts through the richness of the torched Dubliner and the duck fat crust beneath it, while the bitter greens reset the palate between bites. The dijonnaise is not a sauce — it is a binder and flavor bridge between the neutral rice and the assertive SPAM. **Teaching Idea: Flavor Stacking.** A well-built canapé moves through the palate in sequence — fat and tang first (dijonnaise), then salt and umami (SPAM), then heat and smokiness (pepper crust and torched cheese), then freshness and bitterness (greens), then aromatic finish (sesame oil). Each layer has a job. Building in this order ensures no single element dominates.

## Mise en Place (Action Checklist)

- Prepare one full batch of 10-24 Crispy Rice Platform; hold warm on a wire rack
- Combine mayonnaise and Dijon mustard for dijonnaise; hold at room temperature
- Grind the three-pepper blend in a mortar and pestle to a coarse, uneven texture; set aside
- Slice SPAM to ¼ inch thickness; trim each slice to match the footprint of the rice platforms
- Slice Dubliner slightly larger than the rice platform footprint to allow melt-drape on sides (~3 mm thick)
- Wash and dry greens; tear to platform size
- Have torch, sesame oil or olive oil, and sesame seeds at station

## Ingredients

### The Platform
- 14–16 Crispy Rice Squares (see 10-24)

### The Dijonnaise
- 60 ml (4 tbsp) good-quality mayonnaise
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Dijon mustard

### The Protein
- 1 can Korean hot pot-style SPAM, sliced ¼ inch thick and portioned to platform size

### The Sichuan Pepper Crust
- 1.5 g (½ tsp) Dahongpao Sichuan peppercorns
- 3 g (1 tsp) KW Signature Smoked Red Pepper Blend (see Chapter 11)
- 3 g (1 tsp) whole black peppercorns
- Diamond Crystal kosher salt, to finish

### The Melt
- Irish Dubliner, sliced slightly larger than platform footprint (~3 mm thick)

### The Finish
- Baby spinach, mesclun, or mixed bitter greens, torn to platform size
- Extra virgin olive oil or toasted sesame oil, for spraying
- White sesame seeds, to garnish

## Method

**Phase 1 — Build the Dijonnaise**
Combine mayonnaise and Dijon mustard in a small bowl and mix until fully uniform. The ratio produces a spread that reads tangy-forward with body. This is a binder and flavor layer, not a sauce — spread it with intention, not generosity. Hold at room temperature.

**Phase 2 — Grind the Pepper Crust**
Combine Sichuan peppercorns, smoked red pepper blend, and black peppercorns in a mortar. Grind to a coarse, uneven texture — variation in particle size is correct. The Sichuan husks should be cracked open but not powdered. *Sensory cue:* The blend will smell floral and citrusy from the Sichuan, smoky from the red pepper, and sharp from the black. Set aside.

**Phase 3 — Sear the SPAM**
In a dry non-stick or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat, sear SPAM slices 60–90 seconds per side until a light crust forms and surface fat begins to render. *Sensory cue:* Light caramelization on the edges, slightly tacky surface. Remove and hold warm.

**Phase 4 — Assemble**
Working quickly while the rice platforms are still hot and the SPAM is warm:

1. Spread dijonnaise generously on top of each rice square.
2. Place one SPAM slice on the dijonnaise.
3. Season the SPAM surface with the three-pepper blend — enough to coat, not bury. Finish with a pinch of Diamond Crystal kosher salt.
4. Lay one Dubliner slice on top, allowing slight overhang on all sides.
5. Torch the Dubliner until fully melted, beginning to bubble at the edges, with light char marks on the high points. *Sensory cue:* The cheese should flow slightly down the sides and show a few dark spots — this adds smokiness and sets the melt in place.

**Phase 5 — Finish and Plate**
Place torn greens on top of the torched Dubliner. Spray lightly with olive oil or sesame oil — just enough to coat and add aroma. Scatter white sesame seeds. Serve immediately.

## Chef's Notes

- **Greens selection:** Mesclun provides bitterness and texture contrast. Spinach is milder and more neutral. Avoid anything too wet or too delicate — arugula wilts too fast under the residual heat of the cheese.
- **Oil selection:** Sesame oil leans the dish East and amplifies the Sichuan pepper. Olive oil keeps it more neutral. Both are correct — match to context and audience.
- **Sichuan pepper specificity:** Dahongpao (大红袍) is the correct cultivar for this dish. It carries more floral, citrusy character and less aggressive numbing than standard Sichuan pepper. Do not substitute without adjusting quantity.
- **Make-ahead:** Crispy rice platforms (10-24) can be frozen for up to 2 weeks. Fry to order. Everything else assembles in under 5 minutes once the platforms are fried.
- **Service window:** These do not hold. Assemble and serve immediately — the crust softens within 15–20 minutes and the greens will wilt on the warm cheese.

## Glossary

- **Dijonnaise:** A blend of mayonnaise and Dijon mustard used as a spread or sauce. The ratio here (2:1 mayo to mustard) produces a tangy, emulsified binder.
- **Dahongpao Sichuan Pepper:** A premium cultivar of Sichuan pepper (花椒) known for its floral, citrusy aroma and mild numbing sensation (málà). Distinct from generic Sichuan pepper in character and intensity.
- **Maillard Reaction:** A chemical reaction between amino acids and reducing sugars that produces the brown crust and complex flavor on seared proteins and toasted surfaces.
- **Torching:** The technique of applying direct flame from a butane torch to melt, blister, or char the surface of a food — commonly used for cheese, meringue, and caramelized finishes.
