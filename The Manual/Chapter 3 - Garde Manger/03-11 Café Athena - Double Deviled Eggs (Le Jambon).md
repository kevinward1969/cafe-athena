# Café Athena - Double Deviled Eggs (Le Jambon)

Smooth, savory yolk mousse fortified with spiced cured ham and brightened with cornichon brine.

**Yield:** 12 halves | **Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 12 min | **Total:** ~35 min

**Headnote** By incorporating deviled ham directly into the yolk matrix, we transform a rustic pantry staple into a sophisticated mousse. The high fat content of the yolks and mayonnaise softens the aggressive salinity of the cured ham, while the acid from the pickles cuts through the richness. **Teaching Idea: Salt Management via Ingredients.** In this recipe, the ham acts as the primary seasoning agent. We rely on the cured meat's existing salt content rather than adding raw salt, ensuring equilibrium without overpowering the palate.

**Mise En Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Prepare the ice bath:** Fill a medium bowl with 50% ice and 50% water.
* **Pre-boil the water:** Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil — enough to submerge eggs by 1 inch.
* **Sieve the ham:** Pass the deviled ham through a fine-mesh sieve or mash thoroughly with a fork until perfectly smooth.
* **Mince the garnish:** Finely slice chives for the finish.

**Ingredients**

**The Base**

* 6 large eggs (older eggs peel easier)
* 5 ml (1 tsp) distilled white vinegar (for the boiling water)

**The Emulsion**

* 92 g (1 can) deviled ham (3.25 oz standard tin)
* 50 g (3½ tbsp) mayonnaise (see Café Athena — Master Mayonnaise)
* 10 g (2 tsp) Dijon mustard
* 5 ml (1 tsp) cornichon brine or pickle juice
* 1 g (¼ tsp) freshly cracked black pepper

**The Finish**

* 5 g (1 tbsp) fresh chives, minced
* 2 g (½ tsp) smoked paprika (for dusting)
* 12 cornichon slices (optional, for garnish)

**Method**

**Phase 1: The Hard Boil** Add the vinegar to the boiling water. Gently lower the eggs in with a spider or slotted spoon. Reduce heat slightly to maintain a steady simmer (prevents cracking). Cook for **12 minutes**. **Why:** A full hard boil ensures the yolk is crumbly and fully set, which makes it easier to break down into a smooth powder for the filling.

**Phase 2: Thermal Shock & Extraction** Immediately transfer the eggs to the prepared ice bath. Let stand for **10 minutes** until completely cold. **Why:** Rapid cooling shrinks the egg slightly inside the shell, detaching the membrane and making peeling significantly easier. **Sensory Cue:** The eggs should feel ice-cold all the way through before peeling. Peel the eggs, slice them in half lengthwise, and gently pop the yolks into a mixing bowl. Set the whites aside on a serving platter.

**Phase 3: Textural Refinement** Mash the yolks with a fork until they resemble fine yellow sand. Add the sieved deviled ham, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, cornichon brine, and black pepper. Using a whisk or rubber spatula, whip the mixture vigorously against the side of the bowl until it lightens in color and becomes glossy. **Sensory Cue:** The mixture should hold a stiff peak when lifted. If it looks grainy, continue whipping to fully emulsify the fats.

**Phase 4: Piping & Assembly** Transfer the mousse into a piping bag fitted with a star tip (or a zip-top bag with the corner snipped). Pipe generous rosettes into the cavity of each egg white. Dust with smoked paprika and sprinkle with fresh chives. Top each with a cornichon slice if desired.

**Chef's Notes / Variations**

* **The Ham Variable:** The texture of canned deviled ham can be fibrous. If you do not have a fine-mesh sieve, pulse the ham and mayonnaise together in a small food processor to ensure a silky texture.
* **Acidity Adjustment:** If the ham tastes particularly fatty or heavy, increase the cornichon brine by 1 tsp to sharpen the flavor profile.

**Glossary**

* **Emulsion:** A mixture of two liquids that would ordinarily not mix (like oil and water), stabilized here by the lecithin in the egg yolks and the proteins in the ham.
* **Sieve:** To press an ingredient through a wire mesh to aerate it and remove coarse particles.
* **Thermal Shock:** The technique of plunging hot food into ice water to immediately halt cooking and lock in texture.

## Glossary

* **Emulsion:** A mixture of two immiscible liquids, stabilized by emulsifiers like lecithin, creating a homogenous mixture.
* **Sieve:** A kitchen tool with a mesh screen used to strain ingredients, separating solids from liquids and aerating the mixture.
* **Thermal Shock:** The rapid change in temperature applied to food to halt enzymatic activity and alter texture, as utilized in the egg cooling process.
* **Mousse:** A light, airy mixture achieved through aeration and stabilization, often incorporating fats and liquids to create a smooth, velvety texture.
* **Garde Manger:** The French term for the department of a professional kitchen dedicated to preparing cold foods, including appetizers and salads.
* **Textural Refinement:** The process of altering the physical structure of a food, such as mashing yolks to achieve a specific consistency.
* **Peak:** A term used in pastry and sauce making to describe the texture achieved when whipping a mixture, characterized by a firm, stable structure.
