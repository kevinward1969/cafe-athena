# Café Athena - Spicy Sesame & Yuzu Tuna Tartare

## Pristine raw tuna dressed in a vibrant chili-emulsion, served with avocado and crispy shallots

Yield: 4 servings | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 0 min

**Headnote:** We bridge the gap between a Japanese spicy tuna roll and a Mediterranean tartare by using a stable emulsion to bind the fish rather than a simple vinaigrette. This coating protects the proteins from oxidizing while delivering a creamy, lingering heat.

**Teaching Idea: The "Sushi Grade" Reality.** "Sushi Grade" is a marketing term, not a regulation. To ensure safety from parasites, you must source fish that has been commercially super-frozen to -4°F (-20°C) for 7 days. Never freeze "fresh" supermarket fish yourself for raw consumption; home freezers are not cold enough or fast enough to guarantee safety.

**Mise En Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Chill the Gear:** Place a metal mixing bowl inside a larger bowl filled with ice (an ice bath).
* **Sharpen Your Knife:** A dull knife will mash the tuna structure rather than slicing it.
* **Prepare the Board:** Sanitize your cutting board. Ensure no cross-contamination from other ingredients.
* **Toast the Seeds:** Toast sesame seeds in a dry pan until fragrant, then cool completely.

**Ingredients** **The Protein**

* 340 g (12 oz) Yellowfin or Bigeye Tuna (Saku Block, frozen/thawed), pat dry
* 1 Avocado, ripe but firm

**The Emulsion**

* 30 g (2 tbsp) Japanese Mayonnaise (Kewpie)
* 15 g (1 tbsp) Sriracha or Gochujang paste
* 5 ml (1 tsp) Sesame Oil, toasted
* 5 ml (1 tsp) Soy Sauce (Tamari if gluten-free)
* 1 Lime or Yuzu, zest only (save juice for finishing)

**The Crunch**

* 1 Persian Cucumber
* 2 Scallions, green parts only
* 5 g (1 tsp) Sesame Seeds, toasted
* 2 g (½ tsp) Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt

**Method**

**Phase 1: The Vegetable Brunoise** Using your sharpest knife, slice the **cucumber** into thin planks, then strips, then tiny cubes (brunoise). Finely slice the **scallions**. **Why:** Cutting the vegetables first keeps your board clean for the fish and ensures the garnish is ready the moment the fish is cut.

**Phase 2: The Protein Fabrication** Retrieve the **tuna** from the refrigerator. Slice the block against the grain into ¼-inch slabs. Cut the slabs into strips, then dice into ¼-inch cubes. **Sensory Cue:** The fish should look like jewels, not mush. If it starts to look "pasty," your knife is too dull or the fish is getting too warm. Immediately transfer the diced tuna to the **chilled metal bowl** sitting in the ice bath.

**Phase 3: The Emulsion** In a separate small bowl, whisk together the **mayonnaise**, **sriracha**, **sesame oil**, and **soy sauce** until uniform and glossy. **Why:** Premixing the binder ensures even distribution without overworking the delicate fish.

**Phase 4: Assembly** Add the **cucumber**, **scallions**, and **lime zest** to the chilled tuna. Pour the **emulsion** over the mixture. Using a rubber spatula, gently fold the mixture just until the tuna is coated. **Why:** We fold gently to preserve the integrity of the cubes. Add the **salt** and a squeeze of **lime juice** immediately before plating. **Why:** Acid (lime juice) and salt will draw moisture out of the fish and begin to "cook" it (denaturation). Adding them last keeps the texture firm.

**Phase 5: Plating** Dice the **avocado** and place it at the bottom of a ring mold (or bowl). Top with the tuna tartare mixture. Garnish with **toasted sesame seeds**. Serve immediately with nori chips or crostini.

**Chef's Notes / Variations**

* **The Saku Block:** "Saku" refers to a uniform block of frozen tuna loin, trimmed of sinew and skin. This is the safest and easiest cut for tartare.
* **Texture Modifier:** For added crunch, mix in 1 tbsp of crushed fried shallots or tempura flakes just before serving.
* **Acid Control:** Never marinate the tuna in the lime juice. This turns the dish into Ceviche, which changes the texture from silky to firm/chalky.

**Glossary**

* **Brunoise:** A very fine dice (1/8 inch), used to ensure the garnish doesn't overpower the main ingredient.
* **Saku:** A standardized block of frozen fish loin, specifically trimmed for sashimi/sushi use.
* **Emulsion:** A mixture of two liquids that don't normally mix (like oil and water/vinegar), held together by a stabilizer (in this case, the egg yolk in the mayonnaise).
