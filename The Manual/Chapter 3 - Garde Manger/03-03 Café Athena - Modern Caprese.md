# **Café Athena - Modern Caprese**

**A textural deconstruction featuring tomato caviar and balsamic fluid gel.** **Yield:** 4 servings | **Prep:** 45 min | **Inactive:** 1 hr | **Cook:** 15 min

### **Overview**

This dish retains the soul of a classic Caprese—sweet tomato, rich dairy, and acidic vinegar—but completely alters the delivery mechanism. We replace the slice of tomato with "caviar" that bursts on the palate, and the vinegar is transformed into a stable gel that won't run on the plate. **Teaching Idea:** This recipe demonstrates **Direct Spherification**, a process where a liquid containing sodium alginate is dropped into a calcium bath. The calcium ions cross-link with the alginate polymers to form a gel skin from the outside in.

### **Mise en Place (Action Checklist)**

* **Puree and strain** the tomatoes to extract the liquid (tomato water).
* **Hydrate** the sodium alginate in the tomato base (requires blending).
* **Rest** the tomato base in the fridge to **de-gas** (remove air bubbles).
* **Boil and set** the balsamic agar mixture (must cool to a solid block).
* **Prepare** the calcium chloride bath and a rinsing water bath.

### **Ingredients**

**Balsamic Fluid Gel**

* 150 ml Balsamic vinegar (high quality)
* 50 ml Water
* 15 g (1 tbsp) Sugar or glucose syrup
* 2 g (½ tsp) Agar-agar

**Tomato Caviar**

* 600 g (21 oz) Very ripe tomatoes (or canned San Marzano)
* 4 g (¾ tsp) Sodium alginate (0.8% of liquid weight)
* 1 g (pinch) Citric acid (optional, for brightness)
* Salt, to taste
* 5 g (1 tsp) Calcium chloride
* 500 ml (2 cups) Water (cold, for the bath)

**Assembly**

* 250 g (8 oz) Fresh Mozzarella or Burrata
* 1 French Baguette, sliced on the bias and toasted
* Fresh basil leaves (small)
* Extra Virgin Olive Oil (EVOO)
* Smoked salt or Maldon flake salt

### **Method**

**1. Construct the Balsamic Base** Combine balsamic vinegar, water, sugar, and agar-agar in a small saucepan. Whisk to disperse the powder. Bring to a boil over medium heat and hold a simmer for 30 seconds. **Why:** Agar is a hydrocolloid that must be heated to 100°C / 212°F to fully hydrate and activate its gelling properties.

**2. Set the Gel** Pour the hot liquid into a shallow container. Let it cool at room temperature, then transfer to the fridge until it forms a hard, brittle jelly (approx. 30–45 minutes).

**3. Prepare the Tomato Base** Blend the tomatoes thoroughly, then pass through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. You need approximately 400–500g of liquid. Season with salt and citric acid. Sprinkle the sodium alginate over the liquid while blending with an immersion blender. Do not dump it in all at once. **Why:** Alginate is very hydrophilic and will form clumps ("fish eyes") if not sheared into the liquid vigorously.

**4. De-gas the Base** Place the tomato-alginate mixture in the fridge for at least 1 hour. **Why:** Blending introduces air bubbles. If you try to make spheres with bubbly liquid, they will float and be misshapen. The liquid must be clear of air.

**5. Process the Fluid Gel** Once the balsamic jelly is set, cut it into rough chunks. Place them in a blender (or use a tall beaker with an immersion blender). Blend on high until the gel breaks down into a smooth, glossy puree. If it is too stiff, add water 1 teaspoon at a time. Transfer to a squeeze bottle or piping bag.

**6. Spherify the Caviar** Dissolve the calcium chloride in the 500ml cold water (The Bath). Fill a syringe or pipette with the tomato mixture. Drop droplets into the calcium bath. Allow them to "cook" (set) for 45–60 seconds. **Why:** The calcium ions in the bath react instantly with the alginate on the surface of the droplet, creating a membrane. Leaving it longer creates a thicker skin; less time creates a more delicate "pop."

**7. Rinse and Serve** Remove the pearls with a slotted spoon and transfer them immediately to a bowl of clean water. **Why:** This stops the chemical reaction and rinses off the bitter calcium chloride flavor.

**8. Assembly** Pipe a swoosh or dots of balsamic gel onto the plate. Arrange torn mozzarella and toasted baguette slices. Spoon the tomato caviar over the cheese (like a sauce). Garnish with small basil leaves, a drizzle of EVOO, and smoked salt.

### **Glossary**

* **Hydrocolloid:** A substance that forms a gel in the presence of water (e.g., starch, gelatin, agar, alginate).
* **Fluid Gel:** A technique where a brittle gel (usually agar or gellan) is sheared (blended) into a smooth, stable puree that holds its shape on a plate.
* **De-gas:** The process of allowing a liquid to rest so that trapped air bubbles can rise to the surface and escape.
* **Spherification (Direct):** A molecular gastronomy technique using sodium alginate and calcium chloride to encapsulate a liquid inside a gel membrane.

## Glossary

*   **Hydrocolloid:** A substance that forms a gel in the presence of water, crucial for stabilizing the balsamic gel and tomato caviar.
*   **Spherification (Direct):** A molecular gastronomy technique utilizing sodium alginate and calcium chloride to create encapsulated liquid spheres, central to the dish’s textural element.
*   **Sodium Alginate:** A polysaccharide derived from brown algae, used as a key ingredient in the tomato caviar’s gel membrane formation.
*   **Calcium Chloride:** An ionic compound reacting with sodium alginate to form the gel membrane during the spherification process.
*   **De-gas:** A technique employed to eliminate dissolved air bubbles from a liquid, ensuring clarity and proper texture in the tomato base.
*   **Agar-agar:** A seaweed-derived hydrocolloid utilized as a gelling agent in the balsamic fluid gel, providing structural integrity.
