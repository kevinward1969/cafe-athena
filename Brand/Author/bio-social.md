I'm not a chef. I'm a home cook who built a cookbook to organize what I was cooking for my wife Athena — years of recipes scattered across handwritten cards, OneNote files, and photos on my phone that I couldn't use while standing at the stove.

The fix I built to solve that problem eventually became Café Athena: a full manuscript and published website organized around how professional kitchens operate. Technique first. Then stations. Then building blocks — stocks, sauces, dough, the foundations that make everything else work. No culinary school. What I know came from Escoffier, Keller, Pépin, Marco Pierre White, and a lot of time at the stove.

The cookbook is at cafeathenathemanual.com. It's built to be used at the counter — readable on a refrigerator screen, one hand free, something on the stove.
