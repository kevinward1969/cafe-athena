I'm not a chef. I'm a home cook who built a cookbook to organize what I was cooking for my wife Athena — decades of recipes scrawled on index cards, pulled from cookbooks, written on whatever was at hand. The archive went into OneNote, running on an iPad at the counter. Then we got a Smart Fridge with a large built-in display, and that approach stopped working.

I was working through AI certification classes at the same time. The two things converged. The fix became Café Athena — The Manual: a Living Manuscript delivered as an AI-powered website organized around how professional kitchens operate. Technique first. Then stations. Then building blocks — stocks, sauces, dough, the foundations that make everything else work. No culinary school. What I know came from Escoffier, Keller, Pépin, Marco Pierre White, and a lot of time at the stove.

It's at cafeathenathemanual.com. Built for the counter — accessible on anything from an iPhone to a Smart Fridge.
