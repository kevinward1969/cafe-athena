# Writing Exemplars — Café Athena

**Purpose:** Approved paragraphs showing what the correct register looks like. The Writing Director reads this file before any writing task. New entries are added only after Kevin explicitly approves the paragraph.

**Register key:**
- **A** = Acquisition (site copy, About page, social, promotional)
- **M** = Manuscript (headnotes, technique descriptions, glossary)

---

## Author Bio — Personal Narrative

**Register:** A
**Source:** bio-long.md — approved 2026-06-24
**Use for:** Any paragraph establishing who Kevin is as a person and cook

> Kevin is not a chef. He'll say so before you ask — not to manage expectations, but because the distinction actually matters. A chef cooks under pressure, for strangers, on someone else's terms. Kevin cooks for people he loves, in a kitchen he knows, because he wants to. He has been doing this his entire adult life.

**What makes it work:**
- Opens with a declaration that reframes expectations immediately
- Short declarative sentences carrying real weight
- The contrast (chef vs. Kevin) is specific and earned, not generic
- No superlatives, no warmth performance, no marketing cadence

## Author Bio — Culinary Background

**Register:** A
**Source:** bio-long.md — approved 2026-06-24
**Use for:** Describing self-taught culinary education without credential-signaling

> His culinary education happened at family tables, in friends' kitchens, and in books — Escoffier, Keller, Pépin — supplemented by years of footage from Jean-Pierre and Marco Pierre White. He has never staged, never cooked professionally. What he has is a serious reading habit, a lot of time at the stove, and a desire to understand why something works rather than just knowing that it does.

**What makes it work:**
- Names are specific — Escoffier, Keller, Pépin land as credentials without claiming formal training
- "He has never staged, never cooked professionally" — honest, direct, no apology
- The final sentence ("a desire to understand why") is the brand value stated in human terms, not marketing language
- Rhythm: long, list-based opening sentence → two short declarative sentences → one longer closing sentence
